Social Scenarios & Response Templates

When Someone Tags Us / Posts Our Beer

Approach: Confident acknowledgment, build community

Do
  • Reshare to stories with "Campaign-worthy"
  • Comment: "Great taste" or "What's your take on the citrus notes?"
  • Keep it brief, confident, genuine
Don't
  • Over-effusive responses
  • Generic "thanks for the support!"
  • Turn their post into our sales pitch

When Someone Gives Positive Feedback

Framework: Brief acknowledgment + Creative angle or process note

This is why we obsess over every batch. Great to hear the concept landed.

Creative director approved. Seriously though, feedback like this keeps us iterating.

When Someone Gives Constructive Criticism

Framework: Acknowledge + Process/iteration mindset + Confidence

Appreciate the feedback on the bitterness. We're constantly iterating - that's how breakthrough concepts develop.

Good call on carbonation levels. Like any creative work, refinement is endless. Next batch addresses this.

When Someone Compares Us to Another Brewery

Approach: Confident differentiation without disparagement

[Brewery] makes excellent beer. We're approaching craft from a different creative angle - advertising rigor meets brewing obsession. Different stories, same dedication to quality.

When Asked About Price/Value

Framework: Confident position on quality and craft

Obsessive ingredient sourcing, rigorous process, local production. We're not competing on price - we're focused on making campaign-worthy beer.

When Beer Sells Out

Framework: What happened + Next availability + Process note

Batch #[X] sold out. Next cans drop [date]. Like limited campaign runs, scarcity means we're executing at the right scale.

When Asked About Distribution/Availability

Framework: Clear current state + Forward-looking (when accurate)

Currently in Eagle Rock, Silver Lake, and downtown LA. Email jonathan@agencybrewing.com to order or check our locations page. We're growing thoughtfully, not rapidly.

Emergency Scenarios

Quality Issue with Beer

Individual complaint:

Appreciate you letting us know. Every can matters when you're obsessed with quality. DM us and we'll make this right. Your feedback helps us iterate.

Widespread issue:

We've identified a quality issue with batch #[X]. Not the standard we hold ourselves to. If you purchased from this batch, DM for replacement. Like creative work, we own our mistakes and execute solutions.

Can't Meet Demand / Sold Out

Batch #[X] sold out faster than projected. We're scaling production while maintaining obsessive quality standards. Next cans: [date]. Want first access? Email list in bio.

Licensing/Regulatory Questions

Never discuss: Regulatory challenges, specific licensing obstacles, or anything creating legal complications.

Fully licensed through California ABC - Type 09 and Type 17. Questions about our licensing? Email jonathan@agencybrewing.com for details.

Jonathan's Approval Criteria

Pre-Approval Self-Check

Before sending content to Jonathan, verify:

1. Voice Authenticity

  • Sounds like Agency Brewing, not generic craft beer
  • Advertising connection is natural, not forced
  • Confidence without arrogance
  • Creative obsession comes through

2. Strategic Alignment

  • Builds the "advertising × brewing" narrative
  • Focuses on craft and execution
  • Creates desire for the beer
  • Strengthens community connection

3. Execution Quality

  • Concise, impactful language
  • No unnecessary formatting or emojis
  • Free of clichés or tired craft beer tropes
  • Photography/visuals support the copy (when applicable)

Approval Tiers

Approved As-Is
  • Clear advertising × brewing DNA
  • Natural voice, zero "trying too hard" moments
  • Focuses on beer/craft/creative process
  • Visuals and copy work together (Jonathan's call)
Needs Revision
  • Too generic (could be any brewery)
  • Agency speak feels forced or cliché
  • Lacks confidence or sounds apologetic
  • Promises we can't deliver
  • Visual/copy mismatch
Rejected
  • Violates core brand voice principles
  • Sounds like someone else
  • Negative or comparative in wrong way
  • Makes unsubstantiated claims

Fast-Track Approval Items

Once frameworks validated, these need minimal Jonathan review:

  • Standard product drops (Framework 1)
  • Simple customer post reshares
  • Brief behind-the-scenes with 1-2 sentence captions
  • Event announcements using approved templates

Everything else: Full creative director review.

Content Creation Workflow

For Drew: Generating Content

  1. Identify need (product drop, festival, community moment)
  2. Choose appropriate framework from Section 3
  3. Generate 2-3 options using LLM + both brand docs
  4. Self-check against approval criteria (Section 5)
  5. Send to Jonathan with context

For Jonathan: Reviewing Content

Quick Review (Fast-track items)

  • Does it sound like us?
  • Does visual work with copy?
  • Approve or quick edit

Full Review (New campaigns, major announcements)

  • Does this advance our narrative?
  • Is the creative angle compelling?
  • Does execution match concept?
  • Would I approve this as a campaign?

LLM Prompt Templates

Using Agency Brewing Brand Voice Guide and Social Strategy doc, write response to [situation]. Reference appropriate scenario from Section 4. Brief, confident, builds community without over-effusiveness.

Success Metrics & Iteration

Process questions, using our language back

Medium Value

Purchase questions, location inquiries

Low Value

Generic emoji reactions

Every 30 days, review content against Brand Voice Guide:

  • Creative obsession focus (not just beer talk)?
  • Advertising × brewing DNA (not forcing it)?
  • Confidence without arrogance?
  • Community building through shared creative values?

Social media builds awareness and community, but Session IPA has to deliver on the promise. Voice without substance is noise.

We're confident because we're obsessive about quality, not because we're trying to seem bigger than we are.

We're building connections with people who share our creative values, not just collecting followers.
