Brand Messaging

At its core, Agency Brewing celebrates the idea that great work and great beer both benefit from balance, restraint, and intention. This is a beer for people who appreciate quality but don't need it explained, who value culture as much as craft, and who see beer as part of a broader social rhythm - not the main event, but an essential one.

The Brand Lives at the Intersection of

  • Creative and advertising culture
  • Modern social clubs and shared spaces
  • Premium, sessionable, and approachable beers
Each beer is treated as a new brief.

Distinct in expression, consistent in standards. Different styles, same mindset.

Our Audience

  • Light and thoughtful beer drinkers
  • Those drawn to brands that feel considered, not commercial
02

Voice Pillars

Effortless Intelligence

Pillar 1

Short, sharp thoughts. Minimal punctuation. No exclamation points unless earned. Confident but never loud. Clever without winking too hard.

Creative Culture, Translated

Pillar 2

Advertising references used sparingly, only if they land for non-industry folks too. Inside jokes that still feel inclusive. Culture > credentials.

Lightness in All Forms

Pillar 3

Sessionable beer. Sessionable copy. Short sentences. Breathing room. Nothing overwrought.

Social Club Energy

Pillar 4

Feels like being "in," without needing a membership card. Welcoming. Conversational. Community-forward.

What We Avoid

Beer snob language IBUs, hop varietals Long tasting notes Forced humor Meme chasing Slang that ages fast Over-explaining the brand Artisanal Curated Elevated Apologetic language
03

Copy Guidelines

Copy at Agency Brewing is not there to sell first - it's there to set tone, create familiarity, and signal taste. Social copy should feel like overhearing a smart, relaxed comment at the end of a table, not a brand announcement.

Sentence Structure

  • Short lines
  • Occasional fragments
  • White space matters
Bad
  • Our Session IPA is designed for creatives who value balance and quality craftsmanship.
  • This is the perfect beer to enjoy after a long day of work with friends or coworkers.
Good
  • Made for the moment you log off.
  • Work's done.

Punctuation

  • Periods signal confidence
  • Exclamation points are minimal and rare - intentional only
  • Line breaks create rhythm
  • Lowercase is acceptable when it fits the mood

Hashtags

  • Minimal
  • Purposeful
  • Often none at all

Humor & Wit

  • Dry > loud
  • Observational > jokey
  • If it needs explaining, it doesn't ship

Content Frameworks

Product as Campaign 01
[Beer/Batch] + [Creative insight or process] + [Where/When]

Tone: Confident presentation, like launching a campaign

Example

Session IPA. Six months of obsessive iteration, one breakthrough concept. Available now at [location]. This is what happens when creative directors brew beer.

Example

Batch #3 sealed. Like any great campaign, refinement is endless. Same concept, even better execution. Cans dropping this week.

Process as Creative Development 02
[What we're doing] + [Advertising parallel] + [Why it matters]

Tone: Behind-the-creative, transparent about craft obsession

Example

Cold conditioning for two weeks because great concepts need time to develop. Like creative work, rushing the execution kills the idea. Patience equals quality.

Example

Taste-testing batch #4 today. In advertising, you kill your darlings to find breakthrough concepts. Brewing's no different. Iteration is everything.

Community as Collaboration 03
[Acknowledge/Build connection] + [Invite participation] + [Creative angle]

Tone: Collaborative but confident, building creative community

Example

To everyone who grabbed cans this week - your feedback is gold. Great creative work requires dialogue. What are you tasting? Drop notes below.

Example

Festival season starts soon. Come taste what happens when advertising creativity meets brewing craft. Great beer, like great ideas, starts conversations.

Milestones as Campaign Results 04
[What happened] + [Creative interpretation] + [Forward momentum]

Tone: Confident acknowledgment, not celebration for its own sake

Example

Batch #1 sold out. When execution meets concept, people respond. Batch #2 drops next week. This is what campaign-worthy results look like.

Example

First festival pour complete. Watching people discover our Session IPA for the first time - that creative validation drives everything. More events coming.

Differentiation Through Creative DNA 05
[What we do differently] + [Advertising parallel] + [The result]

Use when: Explaining what makes Agency Brewing different

Example

Most breweries focus on tradition. We focus on concept and execution - the same rigor that drives breakthrough advertising. Different DNA, exceptional beer.

Example

Advertising taught us that process drives results. Every batch gets the same attention a creative director gives their best work. Taste the difference.

Quarterly Strategy: 2026

Q1: Tease Phase Jan-Mar

Goal: Build intrigue before full availability. Signals, not announcements.

  • Partial can reveals, typography studies, packaging materials
  • Visual-forward, copy-light

"Something's taking shape." / "Almost ready." / "Details matter."

Q2: Awareness & Placement Apr-Jun

Goal: Be seen in the right places. Show up IRL.

  • Bar and retail placements, spring energy, event visibility
  • Document events quietly - before/after moments, candid conversations

"Good company. Good timing." / "We brought enough." / "Nice crowd tonight."

Q3: Community & Rhythm Jul-Sep

Goal: Feel like part of the routine. Consistently present.

  • End-of-week rituals, summer evenings, familiar tables
  • Repeating formats (OOO Fridays)

"Right on time." / "Same table." / "You know the drill."

Q4: Reflection & Restraint Oct-Dec

Goal: Close the year with confidence and calm. Quieter by design.

  • Low-light, warm visuals. Small gatherings. 6-8 posts/month.
  • Avoid loud holiday language, gimmicks, countdowns

"Same feeling. Different season." / "Still pouring." / "Onward."

Approval Process

Pre-Approval Self-Check

Before sending content to Jonathan, verify:

1. Voice Authenticity

  • Sounds like Agency Brewing, not generic craft beer
  • Advertising connection is natural, not forced
  • Confidence without arrogance
  • Creative obsession comes through

2. Strategic Alignment

  • Builds the "advertising × brewing" narrative
  • Focuses on craft and execution
  • Creates desire for the beer
  • Strengthens community connection

3. Execution Quality

  • Concise, impactful language
  • No unnecessary formatting or emojis
  • Free of clichés or tired craft beer tropes
  • Photography/visuals support the copy (when applicable)

Approval Tiers

Approved As-Is
  • Clear advertising × brewing DNA
  • Natural voice, zero "trying too hard" moments
  • Focuses on beer/craft/creative process
  • Visuals and copy work together (Jonathan's call)
Needs Revision
  • Too generic (could be any brewery)
  • Agency speak feels forced or cliché
  • Lacks confidence or sounds apologetic
  • Promises we can't deliver
  • Visual/copy mismatch
Rejected
  • Violates core brand voice principles
  • Sounds like someone else
  • Negative or comparative in wrong way
  • Makes unsubstantiated claims

Fast-Track Approval Items

Once frameworks validated, these need minimal Jonathan review:

  • Standard product drops (Framework 1)
  • Simple customer post reshares
  • Brief behind-the-scenes with 1-2 sentence captions
  • Event announcements using approved templates

Everything else: Full creative director review.

Photography Guidelines

Our photography is an essential essence of our brand. It showcases the wide range of experiences and hospitality we offer.

Photographic Attributes

Authentic Spontaneous Inspired Connected Nostalgic Contemporary Creative Craftsmanship

Lighting

Daytime
  • Sensorial, reflecting the moment
  • Explore color nuances
  • Silhouettes welcome
Nightlife
  • Hard strobe or high-contrast shadows
  • Less branded, more living in the moment

Subject Guidelines

  • Spaces: Always feel authentic and real, never artificial
  • Experience: Capture enjoyment - mix of close-ups and longer shots for intimate and collective moments
  • Products: Detail shots - pops of color, interesting compositions, authentic feel
  • Events: Capture freedom and energy through sensorial, exciting imagery
  • Locations: Candid, in-action shots of community activities - never staged

Note: Placeholder images should never be used for public-facing communications.
