Copying

How do I make a simple black and white copy?
Place your document (feeder = face up, glass = face down). Press Copy, tap Black, enter the number of copies, and press Start.
How do I make a color copy?
Place your document. Press Copy, tap Full Color, enter the number of copies, and press Start. Note: Color copies may cost more than black and white.
How do I copy double-sided (both sides of the paper)?
Press Copy, then tap 1-Sided/2-Sided. Choose 1→2 to turn single-sided pages into double-sided, or 2→2 if your original is already double-sided. Press Start.
Which way do I put my paper in?
For the top feeder (ADF): Place pages face up with the top edge going in first. For the glass: Lift the lid and place your page face down, aligned to the arrow in the corner.
I put my document in the wrong way. What do I get?
You may get blank pages or upside-down copies. Try this first: Remove your document, check the orientation, and try again.
How do I copy onto legal-size paper?
Press Copy, then tap Paper. Select the drawer that has legal paper loaded. If you are unsure which drawer has legal paper, ask the Reference Desk Librarian.
How do I make my copy bigger or smaller?
Press Copy, then tap Zoom or Reproduction Ratio. Choose a preset percentage or type in your own. 100% = same size, 50% = half size, 200% = double size.
How do I cancel a copy job?
Press Stop on the screen or the physical button. Confirm when asked. Your job will be canceled and any scanned pages will be cleared.
Can I copy a book or magazine?
Yes. Open the lid, place the book face down on the glass with the spine toward the center, and gently press down. Press Copy, choose your settings, and press Start. You may need to copy one page at a time.
Why did my copy come out in black and white when I wanted color?
The machine may be set to Black by default to save costs. Try this first: Before pressing Start, tap Full Color to switch to color mode. Check the screen to confirm your selection.

Scanning

How do I scan a document to my USB drive?
Plug in your USB drive and wait for the screen to say "Found USB Device". Press Scan, tap USB, place your document, and press Scan. When finished, press Job Finish. Wait for the message "USB Device can be removed" before unplugging.
Why won't the machine recognize my USB drive?
Try this first: Remove the drive, wait 5 seconds, and plug it back in firmly. If it still does not work, your drive may be formatted incorrectly. The machine only reads FAT32 or exFAT drives. NTFS drives will not work. Ask the Reference Desk Librarian if you need help.
What is the difference between PDF and JPEG?
PDF is best for documents with text — it keeps multiple pages in one file. JPEG is best for photos or single images. For most documents, choose PDF.
How do I scan multiple pages into one file?
After your first page scans, place the next page and press Scan again. Repeat for all pages. When done, press Job Finish. Make sure Multi is selected (not Single) to combine all pages into one file.
How do I scan to my email?
Press Scan, then tap E-mail. Enter your email address or select it from the address book. Choose PDF as the format. Place your document and press Scan. When done, press Job Finish. Check your inbox (and spam folder) in a few minutes. Note: This feature may not be available at all locations.
My scan never arrived in my email. What happened?
Try this first: Check your spam or junk folder. If it is not there, the file may have been too large. For long documents (more than 10 pages), use Scan to USB instead. If the problem continues, ask the Reference Desk Librarian.
When do I press Job Finish?
Press Job Finish after you have scanned ALL your pages. This tells the machine you are done and saves or sends your file. If you forget this step, your scan may not be saved.
I pressed Scan but nothing happened. What's wrong?
Try this first: Make sure your document is placed correctly (feeder = face up, glass = face down). Check that you selected a destination (USB or E-mail). Then press Scan again.
Can I scan in color?
Yes. Before pressing Scan, look for a color setting and choose Full Color. Note that color scans create larger files and may take longer to email.
What resolution should I use?
For most documents, 200 dpi is fine. Use 300 dpi if you plan to print the scan later or need higher quality. Higher resolution = larger file size.
I removed my USB drive and my file is corrupted. Why?
You removed the drive too soon. Always wait until the screen displays "USB Device can be removed" before unplugging. The machine needs time to finish writing the file.

Printing

How do I print from a library computer?
Open your document and select Print. Choose Cloud Print as your printer and click Print. Note the User ID and Password taped to the bottom of the monitor. Then go to the printer, touch the orange Elevate Sky Print icon, enter the User ID and Password, touch the green Go button, select your document, and touch Print.
Where do I find the User ID and Password?
The User ID and Password are taped to the bottom of the computer monitor (example: "0001"). You will need this to release your print job at the printer.
I don't see "Cloud Print" as a printer option. What do I do?
Ask the Reference Desk Librarian for assistance. They can help you select the correct printer.
I sent a print job but I don't see it on the printer. Where is it?
At the printer, touch the Elevate Sky Print icon and log in. Look for the icon with a number in a white circle — that number shows how many jobs are waiting. If there is no number, your job did not send. Try this first: Go back to the computer, make sure you selected Cloud Print, and print again.
How do I print double-sided?
You can choose 2-Sided when you confirm your print settings at the printer, or set it before printing on the computer. Look for 2-Sided Printing or Duplex in your print settings.
My pages printed sideways. How do I fix this?
Try this first: Before printing, check your document orientation. In the print settings on the computer, select Portrait (tall) or Landscape (wide) to match your document.
My document printed on the wrong size paper. What happened?
The paper size in your document did not match the printer settings. Try this first: Before printing, go to print settings and select the correct paper size (Letter, Legal, etc.).
How do I cancel a print job?
At the printer, touch the Elevate Sky Print icon, log in, find your job in the list, and look for a Delete or Cancel option. On the computer, you can cancel from the print queue before the job is sent.
How much does printing cost?
Black & White: $0.10 (Letter), $0.15 (Legal), $0.20 (Ledger) + tax. Color: $0.30 (Letter), $0.45 (Legal), $0.60 (Ledger) + tax. Pay at the Front Desk immediately after printing. Credit for totals $1.00 or more; cash for any total.

General / Troubleshooting

I pressed the wrong button. How do I go back?
Look for a Back arrow on the screen, or press Function Clear to return to the main menu. You can also press Home to start over.
The machine says "Paper Jam." What should I do?
Do not try to fix it yourself. Please notify the Reference Desk Librarian immediately. Attempting to clear jams can cause injury or damage the machine.
The machine is making a strange noise. Is that normal?
Some noise during printing and scanning is normal. If it sounds unusual (grinding, clicking, or very loud), stop your job and notify the Reference Desk Librarian.
I finished my job. What should I do before I leave?
Take your original documents from the feeder or glass. Collect your copies or prints from the output tray. Press Function Clear or End Process to log out.
The machine says it's out of paper. What do I do?
Notify the Reference Desk Librarian. Do not attempt to refill paper trays yourself.
Can I use my own paper (colored paper, cardstock, labels)?
Special paper requires staff assistance. Please ask the Reference Desk Librarian before attempting to use non-standard paper.
How much does it cost to copy/print/scan?
Pricing varies by library. Check the posted signs near the machine or ask the Reference Desk Librarian for current rates. Scanning to USB is often free.
The touch screen is not responding. What should I do?
Try pressing more firmly with one finger. If it still does not respond, the machine may need to be restarted. Notify the Reference Desk Librarian.
I still need help. Who do I ask?
Ask the Reference Desk Librarian for help with any copier, scanner, or printer questions.
