The Model

Library Applies, Scout Supports

Tredyffrin applies as lead on library grants. Scout is the technology partner. This is standard in library funding — and it means most of the money goes to the library.

How it works

1

Library applies as lead on IMLS, LSTA, and foundation grants — funders want to fund libraries.

2

Scout is the tech partner. Our costs are a line item in the library's grant budget.

3

60–70% of funds go to the library for research, staff stipends, and technology.

4

Library owns the results. The data, the story, and the conference presentations are yours.

For Tredyffrin & Paoli

What the Library Gets

Funded research and staff stipends

Grants pay librarians to test Scout and evaluate results. Your staff get compensated for expertise — money goes directly back to library workers.

Technology and infrastructure funding

Grant budgets cover devices, software, and tools the library keeps beyond Scout — strengthening both branches.

One search for every resource you offer

Patrons stop bouncing between Sierra, Libby, Kanopy, Comics Now!, and the events calendar. Scout unifies them. Twelve languages, voice input, fully accessible.

A tool librarians actually want to use

Librarians use Scout at the reference desk to answer questions faster and pull up resources across systems in seconds. Routine patron questions — "Do you have audiobooks in Spanish?", "How do I access the Inquirer?" — get handled directly, freeing staff for high-value work.

Higher digital resource usage

Patrons underuse Libby, Kanopy, Comics Now!, and news pubs because they can't find them. Scout surfaces these automatically — better ROI on content you already pay for.

Privacy-first AI

Zero query logging. Zero patron data stored. Aligned with ALA's Library Bill of Rights.

The Budget

What This Actually Costs

Scout needs $5,000–$10,000 to pilot at Tredyffrin and Paoli. That's it.

Library (~60–70%)

  • Librarian research & testing stipends
  • Evaluation and patron surveys
  • Technology, devices, and tools

Scout (~30–40%)

  • Development & integration
  • LLM tokens (AI processing costs)

What are LLM tokens? Scout is powered by a large language model (LLM) — the AI that understands patron questions and finds answers. Every query Scout processes costs a small fee called "tokens," similar to how a utility charges per kilowatt-hour. For a library's volume of searches, this runs a few hundred dollars per month.
