# Librarian LLM Beta Client Library Long List

## 15 Ideal Beta Partners for AI-Powered Library Discovery Assistant

## PHILADELPHIA AREA (3 Libraries)

### 1. Free Library of Philadelphia
**Location:** Philadelphia, Pennsylvania

**Community Description:** Serves 1.5 million residents across Philadelphia through 54 neighborhood locations plus central Parkway Central Library. The system reaches one of America's most diverse urban populations with significant African American (40%+), Hispanic/Latino (15%+), Asian (8%+), and immigrant communities spanning low-income, working-class, and affluent neighborhoods. As the 16th-largest public library system in the United States, it provides 6+ million in-person visits and millions more online annually.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** The library demonstrates exceptional technology innovation through its $60 million Building Inspiration initiative, Maker Jawn makerspace program at 5 branches, and recent Google AI workshop series. The system's leadership in accessibility via the Library of Accessible Media for Pennsylvanians (LAMP) serving the entire state, combined with their $11.9M federal Digital Equity Grant (January 2025), positions them as ideal partners for privacy-first, accessibility-focused AI tools. Their independent governance structure, strong intellectual freedom stance, and sophisticated digital infrastructure supporting multilingual services across 300,000+ streaming titles make them perfectly aligned with Librarian LLM's values and scale requirements.

### 2. Chester County Library System
**Location:** Exton, Pennsylvania

**Community Description:** Serves 540,000+ residents across Chester County through 18 member libraries, reaching affluent suburban communities (median income $123,041) as well as diverse urban areas like Coatesville. The system serves a growing county (11.2% growth 2010-2020) with demographics including 76.3% White, 6.4% Asian, 5.3% Black/African American, 8.1% Hispanic/Latino, and 9.6% foreign-born residents across suburban, rural, and small urban communities.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Ranked #136 globally with 1.1 million digital books borrowed in 2023, this system demonstrates exceptional digital adoption with over 25% of circulation being digital materials. Their Innovative Interfaces Vega Discovery Layer provides modern ILS infrastructure ideal for AI integration, while the IMLS-funded Jacobs Technology Center with Digital Media Lab featuring CNC mills, 3D printers, and 9 citizen science kits shows sophisticated maker culture. The comprehensive accessibility commitment with screen reader support, Braille equipment, and 150+ databases—combined with Executive Director Joseph Sherwood's 12+ years of driving digital growth—makes this an ideal suburban beta partner with proven innovation capacity.

### 3. Bucks County Free Library
**Location:** Doylestown, Pennsylvania

**Community Description:** Serves 490,577 residents across Bucks County through 7 county-operated branches and support to 11 additional independent libraries, generating 770,000+ annual visits and 2.2 million items borrowed. The system reaches mixed suburban Philadelphia communities ranging from affluent to working-class townships, serving diverse demographics across the county with over 1,100 new library cards issued monthly.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** The library's recent brand revisualization (2024-2025) explicitly emphasizing "commitment to evolution, growth, and innovation" signals readiness for cutting-edge partnerships. Their Cut Corner Studio maker spaces launched at Bensalem and Quakertown branches feature 3D printers, vinyl cutters, embroidery machines, and comprehensive fabrication tools. The Hamilton G. Disbrow Trust funding provides premium digital resources including Brainfuse, LinkedIn Learning, and Consumer Reports, while CCTV magnifiers at all branches and comprehensive assistive technology demonstrate strong accessibility commitment. As a Pennsylvania public library district since 1979 with modern consortium infrastructure, they offer both innovation culture and stable institutional capacity ideal for beta testing.

## LOS ANGELES AREA (2 Libraries)

### 4. Los Angeles Public Library (LAPL)
**Location:** Los Angeles, California

**Community Description:** Serves 3.86 million residents across Los Angeles through Central Library and 72 branch locations—the largest metropolitan population of any public library system in the United States. The system reaches one of the world's most diverse communities (95/100 diversity score) with 48.6% Hispanic/Latino, 28.3% White, 11.8% Asian, 7.6% Black/African American populations speaking over 200 languages including Spanish, Korean, Armenian, Chinese, Persian, and Tagalog, generating 17+ million annual visitors.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** LAPL leads nationally in digital innovation with 1+ million digital checkouts via Libby/OverDrive topping 2024 national rankings, supported by CARL.X ILS with open APIs ideal for AI integration. The 3,000 sq ft Octavia Lab makerspace (opened 2019) featuring comprehensive fabrication equipment pivoted during COVID to manufacture 126,000+ face shields, demonstrating institutional agility. Their ISO 27001 certification and GDPR compliance for patron privacy perfectly aligns with Librarian LLM's privacy-first approach, while the International Languages Department—"one of the largest and finest multi-language resource centers in North America"—with Transparent Language Online supporting 120+ languages, BrowseAloud accessibility tools, and Video Remote Interpretation services makes them ideal for testing multilingual AI discovery at massive scale.

### 5. Long Beach Public Library
**Location:** Long Beach, California

**Community Description:** Serves 470,000 residents through the Billie Jean King Main Library and 11 neighborhood branches across Long Beach's highly diverse urban community. The system addresses significant equity challenges with 58% of students failing to meet English/Language Arts standards and persistent achievement gaps between white (80% proficiency) versus African-American and Latino students (47-48% proficiency), while serving communities facing high poverty, homelessness, and multilingual needs.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** As the 2017 National Medal for Library and Museum Services winner recognized for "community-collaborations and innovative STEM and literacy-focused programs," Long Beach demonstrates 10+ years of sustained maker innovation through The Studio launched April 2014—predating most public library makerspaces nationally. Their recent migration to Polaris ILS with Vega Discover shows commitment to modern platforms ideal for AI integration, while the Mobile Studio bringing maker programs to all 12 branches monthly demonstrates innovative service delivery models. The library's barrier-free 2022 circulation policy requiring only picture ID and California address eliminates access obstacles, perfectly aligning with equity-focused AI tools serving diverse, multilingual populations through tiered card access options and comprehensive STEM programming developed with Knight Foundation support.

## NORTHEAST & MIDWEST (4 Libraries)

### 6. Cleveland Public Library
**Location:** Cleveland, Ohio

**Community Description:** Serves approximately 385,525 Cleveland residents through participation in the CLEVNET consortium connecting 44 library systems across Northeast Ohio. The urban system serves diverse communities with wide economic disparity and significant immigrant and multilingual populations requiring strong equity-focused services across the Great Lakes industrial region.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Called "the O.G.s in technology" by federal officials, Cleveland demonstrates exceptional AI readiness through their 2023 AI chatbot developed for the John G. White Chess Collection and presented at MuseumNext conference. The $1 million TechCentral reopened May 2025 with 8x expanded makerspace featuring laser engraving, 3D printing, recording studios, and 90+ computers, while the independent IT department established 2022 replaced 600+ computers and upgraded internet speeds 10x systemwide. The innovative FarmBot agricultural technology collaboration and BAND interactive AI-powered robot installation show sophisticated emerging technology integration. With Studio 525 Teen Tech Center focusing on AI, VR, and robotics alongside CLEVNET consortium membership enabling scalability testing, Executive Director Felton Thomas, Jr. and Chief Innovation & Technology Officer John Malcolm provide proven technology leadership ideal for cutting-edge AI discovery assistant piloting.

### 7. Ann Arbor District Library
**Location:** Ann Arbor, Michigan

**Community Description:** Serves 163,590 residents across Ann Arbor School District boundaries in this University of Michigan town with highly educated, tech-savvy population expecting sophisticated digital services. The community features median household income above national average with diverse demographics including significant Asian and international populations characteristic of university cities.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Named Library Journal's National Library of the Year (1997) and maintaining five-star ratings for 15 consecutive years (2008-2022+), Ann Arbor District Library demonstrates sustained innovation excellence. As one of the first public libraries experimenting with video gaming programs and early web-based services, they pioneered user-generated content features including blogs, reviews, tagging, and personal card catalogs. Their comprehensive historical newspaper archives with full-text search, partnership with University of Michigan for academic collaboration, and IMLS grant experience demonstrate sophisticated technical capabilities. Director Eli Neiburger leads forward-thinking programs including tool lending libraries, musical instrument circulation, and Fifth Avenue Press publishing imprint, while the Washtenaw Library for the Blind and Print Disabled administration shows strong accessibility commitment. This medium-large size enables meaningful beta testing without overwhelming scale, while the tech-savvy university community provides ideal sophisticated testing environment.

### 8. Madison Public Library
**Location:** Madison, Wisconsin

**Community Description:** Serves approximately 275,568 residents across Wisconsin's state capital through nine library locations reaching diverse urban and suburban communities. The progressive, civically engaged city features significant University of Wisconsin-Madison population with demographics of 70.3% White, 9.3% Hispanic, 7.9% Asian, 7.1% Black, and median household income of $76,983 in a community highly engaged with government and technology initiatives.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Madison's October 2024 launch of the comprehensive Digital Inclusion Resource Hub in partnership between City IT Department and library system demonstrates current innovation momentum and city-level commitment creating supportive ecosystem for emerging technology. The Bubbler art programming with maker spaces, artist residencies, and exhibitions combined with South Central Library System participation (second-largest in Wisconsin) shows strong regional influence. The library's co-leadership of Wisconsin Book Festival since 2013 with 75+ annual author events and Digital Public Library of America participation with 400,000+ digitized records demonstrates national collaboration capacity. Digital Inclusion Coordinator Cassandra Hill and nine-location network provide multi-site testing capabilities without overwhelming complexity, while the progressive community's strong civic engagement offers receptive beta testing environment for privacy-first, accessibility-focused AI tools with city government backing and university partnership potential.

### 9. Boston Public Library
**Location:** Boston, Massachusetts

**Community Description:** Serves 4+ million annual visitors through Central Library in Copley Square and 25 neighborhood branches across Boston's diverse urban population. Established 1848 as America's first large free municipal library, it serves communities with wide economic disparity including neighborhoods where 46%+ speak languages other than English, reaching significant immigrant populations across one of the nation's most historically important cities.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Boston's 2025 partnership with OpenAI and Harvard Law School's Institutional Data Initiative for AI-enhanced digitization of 5,000+ historical government documents demonstrates exceptional AI readiness and sophisticated approach to AI ethics. This large-scale project scanning 300-400 pages per hour with AI-powered enhanced metadata positions them at the cutting edge of AI integration in library services. The confirmed Polaris ILS (migrated 2012-2013) provides clear integration pathway, while TechCentral with 40 PCs, Connectivity Kits program distributing Chromebooks and hotspots, and BPL Techlab offering 15-hour digital skills training with free Chromebook rewards show comprehensive digital equity commitment. Partnership with Mayor's Office of New Urban Mechanics innovation incubator and Department of Innovation & Technology coordination, combined with teen workshops exploring AI benefits and risks, demonstrates institutional sophistication. President David Leonard and Chief of Digital/Online Services Jessica Chapel lead strategic digital initiatives, while the 200-year collecting history provides rich testing ground for AI discovery across diverse materials.

## SOUTH & SOUTHEAST (3 Libraries)

### 10. Charlotte Mecklenburg Library
**Location:** Charlotte, North Carolina

**Community Description:** Serves approximately 1 million residents across Mecklenburg County including Charlotte and towns of Matthews, Pineville, Mint Hill, Davidson, Cornelius, and Huntersville through 20 library locations. Named Library of the Year by Gale/Library Journal, the system reaches one of America's fastest-growing metropolitan areas with diverse demographics of 39.72% White, 32.5% Black/African American, 16.32% Hispanic/Latino, 7.02% Asian populations.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** The library's comprehensive Digital Strategy adopted by Board of Trustees with $700,000+ investment and dedicated Digital Strategy Manager position demonstrates institutional commitment to technology transformation. Their 2025 migration to Polaris 7.8 with Vega Discover interface provides modern ILS infrastructure ideal for AI integration, while the award-winning MeckTech Program distributing 2,500+ free refurbished laptops and MeckTech Connect partnership providing free public internet shows exceptional digital equity leadership. Recognized as 2021 Top Innovator for Digital Equity and Citizenship by Urban Libraries Council, the library's Idea Box makerspace at Main Library (opened 2015), Knight Foundation grants totaling $804,100+, multilingual DigiLit programming in English and Spanish, and IBM-developed implementation strategy for digital transformation demonstrate sophisticated technical capabilities. Their data-driven approach and strong county funding commitment position them as ideal Southeast partner for testing AI accessibility and localization features with diverse, underserved populations.

### 11. DC Public Library
**Location:** Washington, District of Columbia

**Community Description:** Serves approximately 700,000 DC residents through 26 library locations including the Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial Library central facility across the nation's capital. As an independent agency of DC government with procurement authority, the system reaches highly diverse urban populations with significant Black/African American, Hispanic/Latino, Asian, and international communities concentrated in America's political and technology hub.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** DCPL stands out as the most AI-forward candidate, having launched in August 2025 the nation's first public library AI Upskilling Cohort explicitly focused on AI literacy skills, understanding AI systems, and evaluating AI tools for workforce development. Their recent selection of Polaris with full Vega suite (Discover, Program, Promote) through comprehensive RFP demonstrates modern ILS infrastructure ready for advanced integrations. The Labs at DC Public Library featuring 900 sq ft Fabrication Lab, 850 sq ft Studio Lab, and 1,800 sq ft Digital Commons co-working space hosting DCTech meetups with 500+ attendees shows strong tech community engagement. As IMLS Memory Lab Network leaders providing national personal digital archiving initiative curriculum, they demonstrate grant management capabilities and national influence. The $25,000 Maker-in-Residence Program, 50-70 monthly STEM classes training 500-700 individuals, and capital city location with concentration of tech workers and entrepreneurs provide ideal sophisticated testing environment for AI-powered discovery, while independent procurement authority enables flexible partnership agreements.

### 12. BiblioTech - Bexar County Digital Library
**Location:** San Antonio, Texas

**Community Description:** Serves 2.1 million Bexar County residents—Texas's most populous majority-Hispanic county (59.5% Hispanic, 26.7% White, 7.3% Black/African American)—through 4 branches (South, West, East, EDU) plus satellite locations deliberately placed in underserved neighborhoods. With median household income of $70,571 and San Antonio ranking 60th nationally in literacy despite being 7th largest US city, the library specifically targets the 40% of surrounding households lacking internet access.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** As America's first and only all-digital public library system opened September 2013, BiblioTech represents the most innovative library model in the United States with 100% digital collection perfectly positioned for AI-powered discovery integration. The Polaris ILS + 3M Cloud Library platform with remote Discovery Stations deployable to grocery stores and community centers offers unique opportunity to test AI assistant in non-traditional settings. Serving 36,771+ registered patrons with 600 e-readers, 48 iMacs, 10 laptops, 40 tablets, and cloudLibrary app for iOS/Android/Windows demonstrates sophisticated digital-first operations. The library's mission bridging digital divide with STEM programming including robotics, coding, and 3D printing alongside bilingual Tiny Techolotes Story Time and Mango Languages supporting 61 foreign languages provides crucial testing ground for AI accessibility and Spanish-language features. Services extending to 58 middle/high schools across 14 districts, military bases, juvenile detention centers, and MATCH program for incarcerated mothers show innovative mobile deployment aligned with democratizing AI-powered discovery access.

## WEST & MOUNTAIN (3 Libraries)

### 13. Seattle Public Library
**Location:** Seattle, Washington

**Community Description:** Serves 780,995 Seattle residents within 4.15 million-resident metro area through Central Library and branch locations across the Pacific Northwest's major technology hub. The system reaches diverse urban populations including 8,000+ homeless residents in King County, strong Asian population presence, and multicultural communities with high digital literacy expectations characteristic of Amazon, Microsoft, and tech industry concentration.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Seattle's 2024-2033 Strategic Plan explicitly establishes the library as a "learning lab for experimenting with new technologies," with three strategic foci on individual growth, community impact, and institutional performance. The BiblioCommons catalog interface demonstrates openness to next-generation discovery tools, while Google-funded Wi-Fi hotspot lending, device lending programs, and Finch Robot Program teaching K-12 computational thinking show sustained technology innovation. Comprehensive digital equity program with 450+ annual technology classes, digital navigator services, and partnerships across community organizations positions them as Urban Libraries Council innovation leaders. The 400+ computer workstation Community Technology Center, 30,000+ item zine collection, LinkedIn Learning and O'Reilly access, and privacy-forward approach aligned with Seattle's municipal technology priorities make them ideal West Coast partners. Active involvement in Seattle's One Seattle Data Strategy and history participating in grants (Knight News Challenge, IMLS digital badge system) demonstrate sophisticated capacity for emerging technology partnerships at significant scale.

### 14. Denver Public Library
**Location:** Denver, Colorado

**Community Description:** Serves 700,000+ Denver City and County residents through Central Library and 27 branches plus 2 bookmobiles across Colorado's capital. The highly multicultural system received approximately 40,000 immigrants and refugees in the past year, operating the Plaza Program founded 2008 with 40 dedicated staff providing ESL, citizenship exam prep, and cultural navigation at 9 branch locations serving diverse linguistic communities.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Denver's comprehensive ideaLAB makerspace at the renovated Central Library (grand reopening November 2024) features 3D printers, WhisperRoom recording studio, AKAI MIDI keyboards, laser cutters, Adobe Creative Cloud, and industrial sewing equipment showing sophisticated creative technology culture. The Polaris ILS provides ready integration pathway, while advanced programming curriculum spanning Python (3 levels), web development (HTML/CSS/JavaScript), Micro:bit coding, TinkerCAD, and Fusion 360 demonstrates technical depth from beginner to advanced users. Their recent $80+ million Central Library renovation with upgraded data systems, safety, security, HVAC, and electrical infrastructure creates modern facility ready for cutting-edge technology deployment. The Plaza Program serving 40,000 recent arrivals with multilingual services (strategic plan released in 7 languages) and MakeMake Spanish-language digital content demonstrates exceptional capacity for serving diverse linguistic communities. Strong Library, Strong Denver initiative with dedicated fund, 70+ stakeholder meetings informing renovation, and Community Technology Center with Digital Navigator program position them as Mountain West innovation leaders ideal for testing AI discovery serving immigrant and multilingual populations.

### 15. Boise Public Library
**Location:** Boise, Idaho

**Community Description:** Serves 212,303 residents through main library and four branch libraries across Idaho's capital city, which exceeded 200,000 population in 2008 and continues growing. The system circulates 1,364,517 items annually from 254,319 volumes, serving diverse age groups across expanding suburban and urban neighborhoods in this technology-growing Mountain West community.

**Beta Partner Rationale:** Boise's selection as pilot site for Idaho Commission for Libraries' statewide Make It at the Library initiative positions them as regional innovation leader influencing 172+ Idaho libraries participating in formal maker training, now transitioned to Maker Learning Idaho under Idaho STEM Ecosystem. This mid-sized library provides ideal scale for manageable beta testing with meaningful impact potential and statewide network influence for broader adoption. O'Reilly for Public Libraries access (35K+ books, 30K+ video hours) and LinkedIn Learning demonstrate commitment to cutting-edge technology education, while active participation in SPLAT (Special Projects Library Action Team) innovation group and partnerships with Idaho Commission and JUMP Studios show collaborative capacity. The $80M+ main library renovation plans with Moshe Safdie design (same architect as Salt Lake City Library) signal institutional commitment to modern facilities, while Foundation and Friends group support provides innovation funding capacity. Design thinking methodology experience from maker training aligns with user-centered AI design, making this geographically diverse Mountain West partner ideal for testing beyond major metro areas in growing mid-sized markets.
