45-MINUTE DEMO SCRIPT FOR SCOUT & FINCH

==============================================
PRE-DEMO PREPARATION
==============================================
[ ] Research library's catalog and ILS
[ ] Find 3-5 popular books in their collection
[ ] Note their digital platforms (OverDrive, Hoopla)
[ ] Research recent initiatives or news
[ ] Customize search examples for their community
[ ] Test screen sharing and audio

==============================================
PART 1: INTRODUCTION & CONTEXT (5 minutes)
==============================================
"Thank you for taking time to explore Scout and Finch today. I know how valuable your time is."

"Before we dive in, I'd love to understand [Library Name] better. What's your biggest challenge with patron discovery right now?"

[LISTEN AND TAKE NOTES]

"That's exactly what we're solving. Let me show you how..."

Agenda Overview:
"We'll spend about 15 minutes on Scout for patrons, 10 minutes on Finch for staff, then discuss implementation and our special Librarian Cohort offer."

==============================================
PART 2: SCOUT DEMO (15 minutes)
==============================================
Opening:
"Let me start by showing you what your patrons would experience."

Natural Language Search Demo:
"Watch what happens when a patron types a natural question..."
- Type: "funny books for my beach vacation"
- Show results across formats
- Point out: "Notice it's searching your physical AND digital collections"

Show Format Intelligence:
"See how it shows all available formats?"
- Click on a title
- Show: "Available as hardcover, ebook on OverDrive, and audiobook on Hoopla"
- "No more patrons missing digital options"

Demonstrate Conversation Memory:
"Now watch this..."
- Type: "something shorter"
- "It remembers we're looking for beach reads"
- Type: "any available right now?"
- "Filters to only available items"

Read-Alike Feature:
"Here's what patrons love most..."
- Type: "books like Where the Crawdads Sing"
- Show recommendations with explanations
- "It explains WHY each book is similar"

Let Them Try:
"What's a common request you get? Go ahead and type it..."
[Guide them through their search]

Privacy Emphasis:
"Notice there's no login required. We never store patron searches. Complete privacy."

==============================================
PART 3: FINCH QUICK PREVIEW (10 minutes)
==============================================
Transition:
"Now let me show you what Finch does for your staff..."

Policy Lookup Demo:
"New staff member needs to know DVD loan period..."
- Type: "DVD checkout rules"
- Instantly shows policy with all details
- "No more hunting through manuals"

Onboarding Assistant:
"Here's how it helps with training..."
- Type: "How do I process an ILL request?"
- Shows step-by-step procedure
- "Available 24/7 for evening staff"

Multilingual Support:
"For diverse communities..."
- Show Spanish translation of library card application
- "Real-time translation in 50+ languages"

Custom Procedures (if relevant):
"We can digitize ALL your unique procedures"
- Explain Knowledge Transformation briefly
- "Capture institutional knowledge before retirements"

==============================================
PART 4: IMPLEMENTATION OVERVIEW (5 minutes)
==============================================
Timeline:
"Getting started is surprisingly simple..."

Week 1: "We handle technical setup - just need API credentials"
Week 2: "Customize for your library's needs"
Week 3: "Staff preview and training"
Week 4: "Soft launch with Friends group"
Week 5: "Full public launch"

No IT Burden:
"This is all cloud-based. No servers, no software to install, no IT projects."

Training:
"We provide all training materials, videos, and even marketing templates."

==============================================
PART 5: COHORT OFFER PRESENTATION (5 minutes)
==============================================
Special Opportunity:
"Here's what's exciting for [Library Name]..."

"We're selecting just 8 libraries nationally for our Librarian Cohort."

The Offer:
"You get Scout completely FREE for 12 months - that's a $10,000 value."

"If you want Finch too, it's just $2,995 for the year - an 80% discount."

"After the first year, you lock in preferred pricing at $12,995 for both."

Cohort Benefits:
"But here's what makes it special..."
- Monthly calls with peer libraries
- Direct input into product development
- Your feedback shapes features
- Founding library recognition

Why Free?
"We want to build WITH libraries, not FOR them. Your feedback makes Scout and Finch better for everyone."

Scarcity:
"We already have [X] libraries confirmed, with [Y] spots remaining."

"[Nearby Library] just joined yesterday."

==============================================
PART 6: Q&A AND NEXT STEPS (5 minutes)
==============================================
Address Concerns:
"What questions can I answer?"

Common Questions:
- Integration complexity (it's simple)
- Staff resistance (we help with change management)
- Privacy concerns (we're privacy-first)
- Budget timing (we can work with fiscal years)

Next Steps:
"If you're interested in a cohort spot, here's what happens next:"
1. "I'll send a summary and formal proposal today"
2. "You share with your team for input"
3. "We can do a technical review with IT if needed"
4. "Once you're ready, we reserve your cohort spot"

Create Urgency:
"The cohort is first-come, first-served. We expect to fill all spots by [date]."

"Should I reserve a spot for [Library Name] while you discuss internally?"

Close:
"Thank you again for your time. I'm excited about the possibility of [Library Name] joining our cohort."

"I'll send that proposal right after our call. What other information would be helpful?"
