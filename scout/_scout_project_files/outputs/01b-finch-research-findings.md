# Finch: Market, Product & UX Research Findings
*Supporting Evidence for Librarian Assistant Development*

## Market Research Findings

### Library Workforce Crisis

**Staffing Demographics & Challenges (from context package):**
- **186,500 librarians employed in US** - target user base
- **75% are age 40+, average 47 years old** - aging workforce
- **54% hold master's degrees** - highly educated but underutilized
- **52% are career changers** - often from teaching, need extensive onboarding

**Job Satisfaction Crisis:**
- Job satisfaction declined from 70% (2012) to 56% (2022)
- **#1 complaint: "Lack of time to do everything"** - validates Finch's core value prop
- 73% feel underpaid relative to education level
- 44% rate advancement opportunities as "poor"
- Yet 77% would choose career again - mission-driven despite challenges

**Key Finding:** Librarians are overwhelmed but committed, needing tools that respect their expertise while reducing mundane tasks.

### Workload & Time Management Issues

**Work Environment Pressures:**
- School librarians average 42.9 hours/week
- Often pulled from library duties for other tasks
- 38% voluntarily took on more responsibilities in past year
- Evening/weekend shifts lack senior staff support

**Task Distribution Analysis:**
- Repetitive reference questions consume significant time
- New staff onboarding takes 6-8 weeks typically
- Staff switch between 5-8 different systems daily
- Policy lookups interrupt workflow constantly

**Technology Burden:**
- Expected to be "more fluent in technology and social media" (19% report)
- Assigned functions they're not trained for (16% report)
- 88% offer digital literacy training despite skill gaps
- High confidence with basics, low competence in advanced systems

**Key Finding:** Librarians spend excessive time on repetitive tasks and system navigation rather than professional services requiring their expertise.

### New Staff Onboarding Challenge

**Onboarding Timeline Issues:**
- Traditional onboarding: 6-8 weeks to productivity
- High turnover creates constant training burden
- Senior staff spend 40+ hours mentoring each new hire
- Evening/weekend shifts have no mentor access

**Career Changer Challenges:**
- 52% of librarians are second-career professionals
- Lack library-specific knowledge despite advanced degrees
- Need extensive procedural training
- Make frequent errors in first 3 months

**Knowledge Transfer Bottlenecks:**
- Institutional knowledge held by retiring staff
- Inconsistent documentation across branches
- Policies scattered across multiple systems
- No 24/7 resource for questions

**Key Finding:** Accelerating onboarding could save hundreds of hours annually while improving service consistency.

## Product Research Insights

### Competitive Landscape Analysis

**Current Solutions Inadequacy:**

**General Knowledge Bases (SharePoint, Confluence):**
- Require exact search terms
- No natural language understanding
- Manual updates required
- Not integrated with library systems
- Cost: $5,000+ annually

**Research Insight:** Libraries need intelligent, library-specific knowledge systems.

**General AI Tools (ChatGPT, Claude):**
- No integration with library systems
- Cannot access internal procedures
- No role-based permissions
- Generic responses not library-specific
- Privacy concerns

**Research Insight:** Purpose-built AI for libraries could leverage general AI advances while addressing specific needs.

**Library FAQ Systems:**
- Static, quickly outdated
- Difficult to search
- No integration with workflows
- Manually maintained
- Limited to pre-written answers

**Research Insight:** Dynamic, AI-powered system could provide contextual, updated answers.

### Unique Market Position

**Why No Direct Competition Exists:**
- Library market considered too small by major vendors
- Complex integration requirements deter startups
- Mission-driven sector with unique values
- Requires deep library domain knowledge
- Price sensitivity limits investment

**Finch's Opportunity:**
- First-mover advantage in staff-facing AI
- Can establish category before competition
- Build with librarian input from start
- Focus on augmentation not replacement
- Price appropriately for library budgets

### Integration Requirements Research

**Systems Librarians Navigate Daily:**
1. ILS (Integrated Library System)
2. OverDrive/Libby administration
3. Hoopla management
4. Database administration portals
5. Event management systems
6. Staff scheduling software
7. Incident reporting tools
8. Internal wikis/knowledge bases

**Integration Priorities:**
- Single sign-on capability
- API connections to major systems
- Role-based access control
- Audit trail for compliance
- Mobile accessibility

**Research Finding:** Unified dashboard addressing system fragmentation would save hours weekly.

### Staff Workflow Analysis

**Common Repetitive Tasks:**
- Policy lookups (circulation rules, fines)
- Password resets and account issues
- Technology troubleshooting
- Program registration
- Hold placement across systems
- Incident documentation
- Supply ordering

**Time Sink Activities:**
- Searching for procedures
- Training new staff
- Answering same questions repeatedly
- Switching between systems
- Looking up seldom-used processes
- Finding forms and templates

**Research Finding:** Automating these tasks could free 15+ hours weekly per library.

## UX Research & Design Principles

### Staff User Personas

**Persona 1: New Librarian (Ashley, 28)**
- Recent MLS graduate, first position
- Overwhelmed by unwritten rules
- Needs constant reassurance
- Afraid of making mistakes
- Relies heavily on mentors

**Design Implications:**
- Clear, step-by-step guidance
- Confidence-building features
- Error prevention and correction
- 24/7 availability critical

**Persona 2: Veteran Librarian (Linda, 52)**
- 20+ years experience
- Frustrated by constant interruptions
- Wants to focus on complex reference
- Tired of training new staff
- Values efficiency

**Design Implications:**
- Quick lookup features
- Delegation capabilities
- Knowledge sharing tools
- Minimal interface disruption

**Persona 3: Part-Time Staff (Marcus, 35)**
- Works evenings/weekends
- No senior staff available
- Encounters unusual situations
- Needs policy clarification
- Handles diverse questions

**Design Implications:**
- Complete policy access
- Escalation pathways
- Precedent examples
- Mobile optimization

### Mental Models & Workflow Integration

**How Staff Think About Information:**
- "What's the policy on..."
- "How do I handle..."
- "Where's the form for..."
- "What do I do when..."

**Current Workflow Disruptions:**
- Stop serving patron to look up policy
- Call/text supervisor for answers
- Search multiple documents
- Ask colleagues repeatedly
- Trial and error approach

**Desired Workflow:**
- Natural language query
- Instant, accurate answer
- Stay with patron
- Consistent information
- Learn while doing

**UX Research Finding:** Finch must integrate seamlessly into existing workflows without adding complexity.

### Interface Design Requirements

**Speed & Efficiency Needs:**
- Answers in <3 seconds
- Minimal clicks to action
- Keyboard shortcuts for power users
- Quick action buttons for common tasks
- Search from anywhere capability

**Information Architecture:**
- Progressive disclosure of detail
- Context-aware suggestions
- Related topics linking
- Visual hierarchy for scanning
- Print-friendly formats

**Multi-Device Considerations:**
- Desktop for circulation desk
- Tablet while roving
- Phone for quick lookups
- Consistent experience across all

**Research Finding:** Interface must be instantly intuitive with zero training required.

### Change Management Insights

**Staff Resistance Factors:**
- Fear of job replacement
- Technology intimidation
- Workflow disruption
- Learning curve concerns
- Privacy worries

**Adoption Accelerators:**
- Position as "amplification" tool
- Start with volunteers/champions
- Show quick wins immediately
- Emphasize time savings
- Peer testimonials

**Training Requirements:**
- 2-hour initial session maximum
- Self-paced learning options
- Video tutorials available
- Sandbox for practice
- Ongoing support

**Research Finding:** Successful adoption requires careful change management and clear value demonstration.

## Key Research Conclusions

### Critical Success Factors

1. **24/7 Availability** - Must support all shifts equally
2. **Natural Language Interface** - Match how staff ask questions
3. **Instant Accurate Answers** - Build trust through reliability
4. **Seamless Integration** - Work within existing workflows
5. **Respect for Expertise** - Augment, never replace human judgment

### Market Timing Advantages

- **Staffing crisis acute:** Turnover and shortages at peak
- **Technology maturity:** AI/NLP capabilities now viable
- **Budget availability:** Operating budgets grew 7.9% in 2024
- **No competition:** First-mover advantage in category
- **Generational shift:** Younger librarians expect modern tools

### Implementation Success Factors

**Technical Requirements:**
- Cloud-based deployment (no IT burden)
- API integrations with major systems
- Role-based access control
- Mobile-responsive design
- Automatic updates

**Change Management Needs:**
- Executive sponsorship
- Champion identification
- Phased rollout
- Quick wins demonstration
- Continuous feedback loop

### Risk Mitigation Strategies

**Identified Risks & Mitigations:**
- **Accuracy concerns:** Continuous learning and correction features
- **Job replacement fears:** Clear positioning as augmentation tool
- **Technology resistance:** Intuitive interface requiring no training
- **Budget constraints:** Demonstrate ROI through time savings
- **Union opposition:** Early engagement and transparency
