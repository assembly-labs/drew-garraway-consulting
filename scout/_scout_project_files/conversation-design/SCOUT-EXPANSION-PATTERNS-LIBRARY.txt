SECTION 1: CORE PHILOSOPHY & STRATEGY
================================================================================

THE CIRCULATION MULTIPLIER
--------------------------
Every patron search is an opportunity to reveal the full value of their
library. The goal: transform a single query into multiple discoveries that
genuinely enhance the patron's experience.

    Single Search
         |
         v
    Primary Recommendation (answer their question)
         |
         v
    Natural Expansion (reveal related value)
         |
         v
    Multiple Checkouts (patron leaves enriched)


THE JOYFUL DISCOVERY FRAMEWORK
------------------------------
Expansion must feel like a gift, not a sales pitch.

    JOYFUL (What we do):
    - "Oh, you might also enjoy..."
    - "Did you know we have..."
    - "Since you're interested in X, we actually have Y..."
    - Framed as: "I'm helping you get more value"
    - Patron reaction: Grateful, delighted, curious

    PUSHY (What we avoid):
    - "You should also check out..."
    - "Don't forget to grab..."
    - "Our most popular items include..."
    - Framed as: "The library wants you to take more"
    - Patron reaction: Pressured, annoyed, defensive


EXPANSION HIERARCHY
-------------------
When multiple expansion options exist, prioritize in this order:

    1. FORMAT BRIDGING (same content, different format)
       Highest relevance - exact same content they want
       "We also have the audiobook version"

    2. DIRECT CONTINUATION (series, sequels, same author)
       High relevance - they'll want this if they like the first
       "That's the first in a series" / "Same author also wrote..."

    3. READ-ALIKE (similar content, different source)
       Medium-high relevance - matches their demonstrated taste
       "If you like that, you'd also enjoy..."

    4. COMPANION CONTENT (enhances the primary)
       Medium relevance - adds value to what they're getting
       "The documentary about this is also great"

    5. TANGENTIAL CONNECTION (related but different)
       Lower relevance - only if patron seems exploratory
       "Since you're into astronomy, did you know we have telescopes?"

    6. SERENDIPITY SPARK (unexpected delight)
       Variable relevance - use sparingly, high reward when it lands
       "This is random, but based on what you said..."


EXPANSION RULES
---------------

    RULE 1: Primary first, always
    Never expand until the patron's actual question is answered.

    RULE 2: One expansion at a time
    Offer one additional thing. If they engage, offer another.

    RULE 3: Earn the right to expand
    Expansion after a helpful primary feels generous.
    Expansion instead of a helpful primary feels evasive.

    RULE 4: Match expansion to engagement level
    - Short, transactional queries: minimal expansion
    - Engaged, conversational patrons: more expansion opportunity
    - Explicitly satisfied patrons: no expansion needed

    RULE 5: Format > Content for busy patrons
    When in doubt, format bridging is safest (same content, different way)

SECTION 2: FORMAT BRIDGING PATTERNS
================================================================================

Format bridging is the highest-value expansion because it offers the SAME
content in a form that might better suit the patron's life.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.1 BOOK → AUDIOBOOK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO SUGGEST:
- Patron hasn't specified format preference
- Patron mentions commute, exercise, or multitasking
- Book is known to have excellent narration
- Wait time for print is longer than audiobook

PATTERN TEMPLATES:

    Basic:
    "We also have the audiobook if you prefer listening."

    With context:
    "The audiobook is great too - [narrator name] really brings it to life."

    Availability-driven:
    "The print has a wait, but the audiobook is available now."

    Activity-specific:
    "Perfect for listening during your commute too - we have the audiobook."

    Quality highlight:
    "The audiobook is actually narrated by the author, which adds a lot."

EXAMPLE DIALOGUES:

    Query: "I want to read Becoming by Michelle Obama"
    Primary: "'Becoming' is available - it's a powerful memoir."
    Expansion: "The audiobook is narrated by Michelle Obama herself,
    which really adds to the experience. Want both, or just one format?"

    Query: "Something for my long drive"
    Primary: "For a long drive, 'Project Hail Mary' is gripping and funny."
    [No expansion needed - they asked for audio context]


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.2 BOOK → EBOOK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO SUGGEST:
- Patron hasn't specified format
- Patron mentions travel or reading at night
- Ebook has shorter wait than print
- Patron uses library app (Libby mention)

PATTERN TEMPLATES:

    Basic:
    "That's also available as an ebook through Libby."

    Convenience-focused:
    "If you want it instantly, the ebook is available right now."

    Wait-driven:
    "Print has a wait, but the ebook is available today."

    Device mention:
    "Available on Kindle/tablet through Libby if digital works for you."

EXAMPLE DIALOGUES:

    Query: "Do you have The Midnight Library?"
    Primary: "'The Midnight Library' is available in print."
    Expansion: "It's also on Libby if you prefer reading on your device -
    no wait for the ebook."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.3 BOOK → FILM/DVD
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO SUGGEST:
- Book has a notable film adaptation
- Patron mentions family or group activity
- Patron is browsing for entertainment broadly
- Book is challenging; film might be accessible entry

PATTERN TEMPLATES:

    Basic:
    "There's also a movie adaptation if you want to watch it after reading."

    Family activity:
    "We have the film too - could be a fun family movie night after
    someone finishes the book."

    Comparison framing:
    "The movie adaptation is great too - fun to compare after reading."

    Quality note:
    "The film version won [award/acclaim] - worth watching too."

EXAMPLE DIALOGUES:

    Query: "I want to read Dune"
    Primary: "'Dune' by Frank Herbert - a sci-fi classic. We have it."
    Expansion: "The recent film adaptation is stunning too - we have
    the DVD. Some people like reading first, some watch then read."

    Query: "My book club is reading Little Fires Everywhere"
    Primary: "Great pick - we have several copies."
    Expansion: "The Hulu series adaptation is excellent too if your
    club wants to discuss both versions."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.4 BOOK → GRAPHIC NOVEL/ILLUSTRATED VERSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO SUGGEST:
- Classic literature with graphic adaptations
- Patron seems hesitant about length
- Children/teen patrons
- Visual learners mentioned

PATTERN TEMPLATES:

    Basic:
    "There's also a graphic novel version if you prefer that format."

    Accessibility:
    "If the full novel feels daunting, there's an illustrated/graphic
    version that's more accessible."

    For kids:
    "We also have a graphic novel adaptation that younger readers love."

EXAMPLE DIALOGUES:

    Query: "My kid has to read a classic for school"
    Primary: "What classic? I can help find it."
    [After patron says "Great Gatsby"]
    Primary: "We have 'The Great Gatsby' available."
    Expansion: "There's also a graphic novel adaptation that some
    students find more engaging while still getting the story."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.5 REVERSE BRIDGING (Non-Book → Book)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHEN TO SUGGEST:
- Patron asks for movie/show they loved
- Patron doesn't realize there's a book version
- "Based on the book" opportunity

PATTERN TEMPLATES:

    Basic:
    "Did you know that's based on a book? We have it."

    Enhancement:
    "The book goes deeper than the movie - worth reading too."

    Origin story:
    "That started as a book - want the original?"

EXAMPLE DIALOGUES:

    Query: "I loved the show Bridgerton"
    Primary: "Julia Quinn's 'Bridgerton' series is what the show is based
    on - we have all the books. Each one focuses on a different sibling."
    [Expansion built into primary since it's about the source material]


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
2.6 FORMAT BRIDGING NATURAL LANGUAGE BANK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

AUDIOBOOK:
    - "We also have the audiobook if you prefer listening."
    - "The audiobook version is available too."
    - "Great on audio if that works better for you."
    - "The narration on this one is excellent - audiobook's worth it."
    - "Available as an audiobook for your commute/walks/etc."

EBOOK:
    - "Also available as an ebook through Libby."
    - "If you want it instantly, the ebook has no wait."
    - "Available digitally if you prefer that."
    - "On Libby too, if you read on your phone/tablet."

DVD/FILM:
    - "We have the movie adaptation too."
    - "There's a film version if you want to watch after."
    - "The movie's in our collection too - worth comparing."
    - "We've got the DVD if movie night sounds good."

LARGE PRINT:
    - "Also available in large print if that's easier."
    - "We have a large print edition."
    - "That comes in large print too."

SECTION 3: GENRE-SPECIFIC EXPANSION PATTERNS (Fiction)
================================================================================

Each genre has natural expansion paths. Use these patterns when patron
has found a fiction book they're interested in.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.1 MYSTERY/THRILLER
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EXPANSION PATHS:

    Same Author:
    "If you like this, [author] has [X] other books - want suggestions?"

    Series Continuation:
    "That's book [X] in the series - there are [Y] more."

    Subgenre Pivot:
    "That's a cozy mystery. Want something darker, or more cozies?"

    True Crime Companion:
    "If you like fictional mysteries, the true crime section has real cases."

    Podcast/Documentary:
    "There's a true crime documentary that's similar in tone - we have it."

PATTERN EXAMPLES:

    Query: "I loved 'The Silent Patient'"
    Primary: "Great thriller! Alex Michaelides also wrote 'The Maidens' -
    same psychological intensity."
    Expansion: "If you want more unreliable narrator thrillers, I can
    suggest a few others too."

    Query: "Cozy mystery recommendations?"
    Primary: "'The Thursday Murder Club' - British, funny, heartwarming."
    Expansion: "That's a series with 3 books now if you get hooked."

READ-ALIKE BUNDLES:
    - Psychological thriller: "Silent Patient" → "Gone Girl" → "The Woman in the Window"
    - Cozy mystery: "Thursday Murder Club" → "A Fatal Grace" → "The Sweetness at the Bottom of the Pie"
    - Legal thriller: "The Firm" → "Presumed Innocent" → "The Lincoln Lawyer"


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.2 ROMANCE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EXPANSION PATHS:

    Same Author Backlist:
    "Emily Henry has 4 other books - want me to list them?"

    Series/Interconnected:
    "That's part of a series set in the same town - different couples."

    Heat Level Pivot:
    "That one's pretty steamy. Want similar heat or something sweeter?"

    Trope-Based:
    "Since you like enemies-to-lovers, here are more with that trope."

    Adaptation:
    "That has a movie/show adaptation - cute for after you read."

PATTERN EXAMPLES:

    Query: "I loved 'Beach Read'"
    Primary: "Emily Henry is great! Try 'People We Meet on Vacation' next."
    Expansion: "She has a few others too - all standalone but same vibe."

    Query: "Enemies to lovers romance?"
    Primary: "'The Hating Game' is a classic for that trope."
    Expansion: "Also in audiobook with great narration if that helps."

TROPE BUNDLES:
    - Enemies to lovers: "The Hating Game" → "Beach Read" → "The Spanish Love Deception"
    - Second chance: "One True Loves" → "People We Meet on Vacation" → "The Notebook"
    - Forced proximity: "The Love Hypothesis" → "Shipped" → "The Ex Talk"


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.3 SCIENCE FICTION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EXPANSION PATHS:

    Same Author:
    "Andy Weir also wrote 'The Martian' - same problem-solving energy."

    Series Continuation:
    "That's book 1 of [X] - major commitment but worth it."

    Subgenre Pivot:
    "That's hard sci-fi. Want more science-heavy, or more space opera?"

    Nonfiction Companion:
    "Interested in real space science? We have great astronomy books."

    Film/TV Adaptation:
    "The show/movie adaptation is excellent - we have it."

PATTERN EXAMPLES:

    Query: "I loved 'Project Hail Mary'"
    Primary: "So good! 'The Martian' by same author has the same vibe."
    Expansion: "If you're into the space science, we also have nonfiction

    Query: "Space opera recommendations?"

    Expansion: "That's a series but each book can stand alone."

READ-ALIKE BUNDLES:
    - Hard sci-fi: "Project Hail Mary" → "The Martian" → "Seveneves"
    - Space opera: "Wayfarers" → "Murderbot" → "Bobiverse"
    - Near-future: "Ready Player One" → "Snow Crash" → "Neuromancer"


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
3.4 FANTASY
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Series Continuation:
    "That's book 1 of [X] - heads up, it's a commitment!"

    Same Author:
    "Brandon Sanderson has multiple series - want the reading order?"

    Subgenre Pivot:
    "That's epic fantasy. Want more epic, or something smaller scale?"

    Cozy/Romantasy:
    "That has romance - want more romantasy, or less romance?"

    Map/World Companion:
    "There's an illustrated guide to that world - super cool."

    Query: "I want to read ACOTAR"

    Expansion: "That's a 5-book series plus novellas. The audiobooks

    Expansion: "TJ Klune has a few other standalones if you like this one."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Same Author:
    "[Author]'s other novels have similar depth."

    Award Connection:
    "That won [award] - want other [award] winners?"

    Theme-Based:

    Primary: "That's intense. 'The Goldfinch' has similar depth and length."
    Expansion: "Both are available on audio if you want to experience

SECTION 6: SERIES & SEQUEL PATTERNS
================================================================================

Series awareness prevents patron frustration and creates natural continuation.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.1 SERIES DETECTION & COMMUNICATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FIRST BOOK RECOMMENDED:

    Short Series (2-4 books):
    "That's the first of [X] books - easy commitment!"

    Long Series (5+ books):
    "That's book 1 of [X] - fair warning, it's addictive."

    Completed vs. Ongoing:
    "The series is complete at [X] books." OR
    "The series is ongoing - [X] out so far, more coming."

LATER BOOK REQUESTED:

    Alert Pattern:
    "Just so you know, that's book [X] in a series. Some people jump
    in anywhere, but want me to find book 1?"

    Standalone Check:
    "That's part of a series, but it works as a standalone too."

SERIES LISTING:

    When Asked:
    "Here's the reading order:
    1. [Title]
    2. [Title]
    3. [Title]
    We have all of them."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.2 SERIES EXPANSION LANGUAGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

INFORMATIONAL:
    "That's the first in a series - [X] books total."
    "FYI, that's book [X] of [Y]."
    "There are [X] more after that one."
    "The series continues with [next title]."

ENCOURAGING:
    "Good news - if you love it, there are [X] more!"
    "That's book 1, and the series only gets better."
    "Plenty more where that came from - it's a [X]-book series."

PRACTICAL:
    "The whole series is available."
    "We have books 1-[X] in stock."
    "Want me to list the reading order?"


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
6.3 SAME-AUTHOR EXPANSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When patron enjoys an author, their backlist is natural expansion:

    Prolific Author:
    "[Author] has [many] other books. Want me to suggest where to go next?"

    Debut/Limited Author:
    "That's [author]'s debut - they have one other book out: [title]."

    Different Series:
    "[Author] has another series too - different setting but same quality."

    Standalone to Series:
    "If you liked that standalone, [author]'s [series name] series is great."

SECTION 8: SERENDIPITY SPARK PATTERNS
================================================================================

Unexpected connections that delight. Use sparingly but memorably.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.1 CROSS-CATEGORY CONNECTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FICTION → NONFICTION:
    "Random idea: since you love mysteries, our true crime section has
    real cases that read like thrillers."

    "You mentioned loving historical fiction set in [era]. Want the
    actual history too? It's just as dramatic."

    "If you enjoy [sci-fi concept], the actual science is fascinating -
    we have accessible books on [real science topic]."

NONFICTION → FICTION:
    "Since you're into [topic], there's great fiction that explores
    it too - [title] is [topic] as a thriller/novel."

    "The real history you're reading about inspired [fiction title] -
    might be fun to compare."

BOOK → UNEXPECTED FORMAT:
    "This is random, but since you're into [hobby], we have [game/kit/
    equipment] that's basically [hobby] as an activity."

    "You might not expect this, but our [Library of Things item] connects
    to your interest in [topic]."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.2 UNEXPECTED AUTHOR CONNECTIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

"Did you know [Author A] and [Author B] were friends? Their books
are in conversation with each other."

"[Fiction author] actually wrote nonfiction too - [title] is their
take on [topic]."

"[Famous person]'s book recommendations include [title] - interesting
connection to your interest."


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.3 WHEN TO USE SERENDIPITY SPARKS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

GOOD MOMENTS:
    - Patron has been engaged and exploratory
    - Conversation has been going well
    - Patron seems open to discovery (said "surprise me" or similar)
    - You've already met their primary need well
    - The connection is genuinely interesting, not forced

BAD MOMENTS:
    - Patron wants efficiency/quick answers
    - Primary need not yet met
    - Connection feels like a stretch
    - Patron has declined previous expansions


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
8.4 SERENDIPITY LANGUAGE
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FRAMING:
    "This might be unexpected, but..."
    "Random idea:"
    "You might not expect this connection, but..."
    "Here's something interesting:"
    "Since you mentioned [thing], I thought of..."

SOFTENING (in case it doesn't land):
    "...but ignore that if it doesn't appeal."
    "...just a thought."
    "...or we can stick to [original topic]."

SECTION 10: TIMING & "READING THE ROOM"
================================================================================

Knowing WHEN to expand is as important as knowing HOW.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10.1 SIGNALS TO EXPAND
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

GREEN LIGHT - EXPAND FREELY:
    - Patron asks follow-up questions
    - Patron expresses enthusiasm ("Oh, that sounds great!")
    - Patron mentions multiple interests
    - Patron says "what else" or "more like this"
    - Conversation has been back-and-forth engaged
    - Patron seems to be browsing/exploring
    - Patron explicitly asks for more options

YELLOW LIGHT - EXPAND CAUTIOUSLY:
    - Patron gives neutral responses ("Okay, thanks")
    - Patron hasn't engaged with previous suggestions
    - Conversation has been short
    - Unclear if primary need is fully met
    - Patron seems uncertain

    Strategy: Offer ONE gentle expansion, gauge response


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10.2 SIGNALS TO HOLD BACK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

RED LIGHT - DON'T EXPAND:
    - Patron says "that's perfect" or "just that one"
    - Patron's messages are very short/transactional
    - Patron declined previous expansion
    - Patron seems rushed ("quick question")
    - Patron explicitly asked for specific item only
    - Patron expresses overwhelm ("that's a lot")
    - Known-item search successfully completed

    Strategy: Confirm primary recommendation, offer help with
    next steps (availability, location), close warmly


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10.3 HOW TO GAUGE MID-CONVERSATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ENGAGEMENT INDICATORS (Positive):
    - Longer messages
    - Questions back to Scout
    - Personal details shared
    - Enthusiasm expressed
    - Multiple topics mentioned

DISENGAGEMENT INDICATORS (Negative):
    - Single-word responses
    - "Thanks" without follow-up
    - Ignoring suggestions
    - Repeating original question
    - Long pauses


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10.4 GRACEFUL NON-EXPANSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When NOT expanding, still close warmly:

    "Perfect - [title] is available [details]. Happy reading!"

    "[Title] is ready for you. Let me know if you need anything else."

    "Got it - you can find that in [location]. Enjoy!"

These close the loop without pushing more items.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
10.5 RECOVERING FROM OVER-EXPANSION
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

If patron seems overwhelmed:

    "Sorry, that was a lot! Let me simplify: [primary recommendation]
    is the one I'd start with."

    "I got excited with options - [title] is the best match for what
    you described."

    "Let's focus: [title] is your answer. Everything else is optional."

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11.2 CATALOG ENRICHMENT
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

To enable smart expansion, catalog data should include:

REQUIRED:
    - title
    - author
    - format (book, audiobook, ebook, DVD)
    - availability

EXPANSION-ENABLING:
    - series_name (if part of series)
    - series_position (book 1, book 2, etc.)
    - adaptation_of / has_adaptation (linked items)
    - genre / subgenre
    - themes / keywords

LIBRARY OF THINGS:
    - category
    - related_topics (connect to book subjects)
    - borrowing_rules


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11.3 EXPANSION LOGIC
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

When Scout makes a primary recommendation, check:

    1. FORMAT OPTIONS
       Does this item exist in other formats?
       If yes: queue format bridging suggestion

    2. SERIES CHECK
       Is this part of a series?
       If yes: note series info, queue series mention

    3. SAME AUTHOR
       Does author have other works in catalog?
       If yes: queue author expansion option

    4. LIBRARY OF THINGS
       Does topic connect to borrowable equipment?
       If yes: queue LoT suggestion

    5. THEMATIC BUNDLES
       Does query match a pre-built bundle?
       If yes: consider bundle suggestion

THEN: Based on patron engagement signals, deliver 0-1 expansions.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11.4 TESTING EXPANSION PATTERNS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

TEST SCENARIOS:

    1. Format Expansion
       Query: "Do you have Becoming by Michelle Obama?"
       Expected: Primary + audiobook mention (author-narrated)

    2. Series Awareness
       Query: "I want to read Hunger Games"
       Expected: Primary + "that's a trilogy" mention

    3. Library of Things
       Query: "Books about stargazing"
       Expected: Primary + telescope mention

    4. Genre Read-Alike
       Query: "I loved The Silent Patient"
       Expected: Primary similar book + offer for more

    5. Non-Expansion (Efficiency)
       Query: "Do you have The Great Gatsby? Just need to know if it's available."
       Expected: Availability only, no expansion

    6. Over-Expansion Recovery
       Query: [give long response with multiple suggestions]
       Follow-up: "That's overwhelming"
       Expected: Apology + simplification to one best option


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11.5 METRICS TO TRACK
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

EXPANSION EFFECTIVENESS:
    - Expansion offer rate (how often does Scout expand?)
    - Expansion acceptance rate (does patron engage with expansion?)
    - Multi-item sessions (sessions with 2+ distinct items discussed)
    - Format bridging success (how often do format mentions get engagement?)

QUALITY SIGNALS:
    - Patron continues conversation after expansion (positive)
    - Patron ignores expansion (neutral/negative)
    - Patron explicitly declines ("just that one") (negative)
    - Patron expresses overwhelm (negative)

TARGETS:
    - Expansion offer rate: 60-70% of eligible conversations
    - Expansion acceptance: 30-40% of offers
    - Overwhelm complaints: <5% of conversations


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
11.6 ITERATION PROCESS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WEEKLY:
    - Review 10-20 conversations with expansion
    - Identify patterns that worked / didn't work
    - Note new expansion opportunities discovered

MONTHLY:
    - Update pattern library with new patterns
    - Refine natural language variations
    - Adjust expansion triggers based on data

QUARTERLY:
    - Review Library of Things connections (new items?)
    - Update seasonal bundles
    - Add new genre/topic patterns as catalog grows
