## Interview Script

### Opening (5 min)

**Introduction:**
> "Thanks for taking the time to talk with me today. I'm [name], and I'm researching [topic area]. There are no right or wrong answers—I'm just trying to understand how you [relevant activity]. This should take about [duration]."

**Permission:**
> "Is it okay if I record this? It's just for my notes and won't be shared publicly."

**Warm-up question:**
> "To start, can you tell me a bit about yourself and your role/situation?"

---

### Context & Background (10 min)

*Understand their world before diving into the problem.*

1. "Tell me about your typical [day/week/process] when it comes to [relevant activity]."
   - *Listen for: behaviors, tools, frequency, frustrations*

2. "What tools or methods do you currently use for [relevant task]?"
   - *Listen for: current solutions, workarounds*

3. "How long have you been doing [activity]? How has it changed?"
   - *Listen for: evolution, pain points that have emerged*

---

### Problem Exploration (15 min)

*Dig into the problem space. Don't lead—let them reveal pain points.*

4. "What's the hardest part about [relevant activity]?"
   - *Follow up: "Can you tell me about a specific time that happened?"*

5. "When was the last time you felt frustrated with [relevant task]?"
   - *Follow up: "What happened? What did you do?"*

6. "If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about [area], what would it be?"
   - *Listen for: priorities, intensity of pain*

7. "How do you work around [problem mentioned]?"
   - *Listen for: current solutions, effort invested*

8. "What happens when [problem] occurs? What's the impact?"
   - *Listen for: consequences, severity*

---

### Solution Exploration (10 min)

*Understand their mental model of solutions—don't pitch your idea.*

9. "Have you tried any tools or approaches to solve [problem]?"
   - *Follow up: "What worked? What didn't?"*

10. "What would an ideal solution look like for you?"
    - *Listen for: features, expectations, mental model*

11. "What would make you switch from your current approach to something new?"
    - *Listen for: switching costs, motivators*

---

### Validation (5 min)

*If you have a concept, test it carefully—don't sell.*

12. "I'm exploring an idea for [brief description]. What's your initial reaction?"
    - *Listen for: enthusiasm, skepticism, confusion*

13. "What questions or concerns come to mind?"
    - *Listen for: objections, risks, gaps in your thinking*

14. "Would this be useful to you? Why or why not?"
    - *Watch for: polite "yes" vs. genuine interest*

---

### Wrap-up (5 min)

15. "Is there anything else about [topic] that I should have asked?"
    - *Often reveals important insights*

16. "Who else should I talk to about this?"
    - *Snowball recruiting*

17. "Would you be open to a follow-up conversation as we develop this further?"

**Closing:**
> "Thank you so much for your time. This has been incredibly helpful. [If applicable: Here's how to reach me if anything else comes to mind.]"

## During the Interview

### Interviewing Tips

**Do:**
- Ask "why" and "tell me more"
- Let silence sit—they'll often fill it with insights
- Follow interesting threads even if off-script
- Note body language and tone
- Ask for specific examples ("Last time that happened...")

**Don't:**
- Lead with your solution
- Ask yes/no questions
- Interrupt or finish sentences
- Get defensive about your idea
- Take notes so intensely you lose eye contact

### Useful Follow-up Phrases
- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "What do you mean by [term they used]?"
- "Can you walk me through the last time that happened?"
- "Why is that important to you?"
- "What would happen if you didn't do that?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
