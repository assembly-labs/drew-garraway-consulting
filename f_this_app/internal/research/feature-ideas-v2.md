## Feature 1: Streak Freeze & Recovery Mechanics

### The Idea
Allow players to earn or purchase "streak freezes" that protect their clean streak from a single slip-up. Similar to Duolingo's streak freeze, but earned through positive behavior (using nice words, going a full week clean) rather than just paying.

### Why Build This

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Retention** | Duolingo's data shows users are **2.3x more likely** to engage daily once they have a 7-day streak. Streaks become identity. Breaking one causes rage-quits. |
| **Engagement Loop** | Creates new goals: "I need 2 more clean days to earn a freeze" |
| **Monetization** | Streak freezes can be premium ($0.99) or earned ‚Äî classic freemium hook |
| **Competitive Advantage** | No curse-tracking competitor offers this |

### Use Cases
- Parent who slipped up at a frustrating school pickup but had a 14-day streak
- Office player who cursed during a heated Slack thread but earned protection
- Weekend warrior who wants insurance before a drinking night

**Priority:** HIGH ‚Äî Directly addresses churn from broken streaks

## Feature 2: Dispute & Challenge System

### The Idea
Allow reported players to dispute a curse report. The reporter must provide context or a witness. Group voting or referee system resolves disputes. Failed challenges cost the challenger points.

### Why Build This

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Fairness** | Prevents trolling and false reporting in competitive games |
| **Engagement** | Disputes create drama and talking points ‚Äî social glue |
| **Trust** | Research shows referee systems increase success rates by **78%** (Stickk data) |
| **Depth** | Adds strategic layer: "Is it worth disputing this?" |

### Use Cases
- Player who was reported for saying "ship" but it sounded like something else
- Competitive game where friends test the honor system limits
- Family game where kids report parents too eagerly

**Priority:** MEDIUM ‚Äî Important for competitive integrity as games scale

## Feature 3: Curse Severity Tiers

### The Idea
Not all curses are equal. Introduce three tiers:
- **Mild** (damn, hell, crap): +1 point
- **Moderate** (common curse words): +2 points
- **Nuclear** (the really bad ones): +3 points

Players select severity when reporting. Creates risk/reward for the reporter (over-reporting gets pushback).

### Why Build This

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Realism** | Reflects how people actually think about language |
| **Strategy** | Adds decision-making: "Was that a +1 or +2?" |
| **Fairness** | Stubbing your toe vs. road rage shouldn't be equal |
| **Engagement** | Group debates about severity = content |

### Use Cases
- Office game that wants to penalize F-bombs more than "dammit"
- Family game that treats "stupid" differently than actual curses
- Friend group with inside jokes about specific words

**Priority:** MEDIUM ‚Äî Adds depth but increases complexity

## Feature 7B: Consequence Contracts & Loser Dares

### The Idea
Before a game starts, players collectively agree on **consequences for the loser(s)**. These can be:

**Tier 1 ‚Äî Silly/Social:**
- Dye hair a color chosen by the winner
- Wear a ridiculous outfit to work/school for a day
- Post an embarrassing photo chosen by the group
- Sing karaoke at the next gathering (song chosen by winner)
- Change social media profile pic to whatever winner chooses (1 week)

**Tier 2 ‚Äî Service:**
- Buy winner(s) dinner/drinks
- Do winner's chores for a week
- Be the designated driver for a month
- Make breakfast for the family for a week

**Tier 3 ‚Äî Public Accountability:**
- Public apology video posted to stories
- Wear a "I have a potty mouth" t-shirt for a day
- Let winner write a social media post from your account

**How It Works:**
1. During game setup, host selects or creates consequence
2. All players must "sign" (tap to agree) before game starts
3. Consequence is locked and visible throughout game
4. At game end, loser is reminded of their commitment
5. Winner can "mark as completed" with optional photo/video proof
6. Hall of Fame gallery of completed consequences (opt-in)

### Why Build This

| Factor | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| **Lower Barrier** | Not everyone wants to risk money, but EVERYONE fears public embarrassment |
| **Viral Gold** | "Watch Alex dye his hair pink because he lost Swear Jar" = CONTENT |
| **Social Pressure** | Knowing the consequence makes every curse report feel weightier |
| **Shareability** | Proof photos/videos of consequences are inherently shareable |
| **Differentiation** | No habit app combines behavior tracking with dare mechanics |
| **Humor** | Aligns perfectly with the playful, roasting brand voice |
| **Trust System** | Pre-commitment + social witnesses = high follow-through |

### Psychology Behind It

The research on commitment devices shows:
- **Public commitment** increases follow-through by 65% vs private goals
- **Social witnesses** act as accountability pressure even without financial stakes
- **Loss aversion** applies to social capital (reputation) not just money
- **Pre-commitment** (agreeing upfront) eliminates wiggle room later

This is essentially **Truth or Dare meets habit tracking** ‚Äî and no one has done it.

### Use Cases

| Scenario | Consequence Example |
|----------|---------------------|
| **Office Game** | Loser buys the team lunch; wears team jersey of winner's choice |
| **Friend Group** | Loser gets a temporary tattoo chosen by winner; does karaoke solo |
| **Couples** | Loser plans and pays for date night; does dishes for a month |
| **Family** | Kids: extra chores for a week; Parents: let kids pick dinner for a week |
| **Roommates** | Loser cleans the bathroom for a month |
| **Fitness Buddy Group** | Loser has to do 100 burpees on video |

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|

| **Bullying potential** | All players must agree; can dispute unfair demands |
| **Legal liability** | Clear terms that we're just tracking, not enforcing |

### Combining with Money Stakes

The killer feature: **Money + Consequences**

- Winner gets the pot AND loser does the dare
- Or: Consequences INSTEAD of money (lower barrier)
- Or: Buy your way out of consequence (winner gets the escape fee)
