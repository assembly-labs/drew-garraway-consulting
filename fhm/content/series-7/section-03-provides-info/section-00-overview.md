# Section 3: Provides Info, Makes Recommendations, Transfers Assets

## This Is The Exam

Let's be direct: Section 3 is 73% of your Series 7. That's 91 out of 125 questions. Master this section and you pass. Struggle here and no amount of excellence in the other sections will save you.

This is where product knowledge lives—options, bonds, mutual funds, margin accounts, municipal securities. Everything a General Securities Representative needs to know about the products they can sell.

---

## What You'll Learn

### Equity Securities (8-10%)
Common stock, preferred stock, rights, warrants, ADRs. The foundation of securities knowledge.

### Debt Securities (10-12%)
Corporate bonds, government securities, yield calculations, interest rate risk. Understanding how fixed income works.

### Municipal Bonds (15-20%)
GO bonds, revenue bonds, MSRB rules, tax advantages. One of the most heavily tested areas.

### Options (25-30%)
**The biggest single topic on your exam.** Calls, puts, strategies, spreads, calculations. Max gain, max loss, breakeven—you'll calculate these in your sleep.

### Investment Companies (5-7%)
Mutual funds, closed-end funds, ETFs, UITs. NAV calculations, sales charges, share classes.

### Variable Products (5-7%)
Variable annuities, variable life insurance. Accumulation, annuitization, death benefits.

### Retirement Plans (5-8%)
401(k)s, IRAs, Roth accounts. Contribution limits, distribution rules, ERISA requirements.

### Margin Accounts (10-15%)
Regulation T, long margin, short margin. SMA calculations, buying power, maintenance calls. **Calculation-heavy.**

### DPPs (3-5%)
Limited partnerships, tax implications, suitability considerations.

### Other Products (3-5%)
REITs, hedge funds, ETNs. Characteristics and risks.

### Analysis & Economics (3-5%)
Fundamental analysis, technical analysis, economic indicators.

- Options spreads and advanced strategies

- Debt securities and yields

### Calculation Priority
If you're short on time, prioritize calculation mastery:
1. Options max gain/loss/breakeven
2. Margin SMA and buying power

## The Calculation Challenge

Section 3 is where calculations live. You cannot memorize your way through:

**Options calculations you'll see:**
- Maximum gain for each position
- Maximum loss for each position
- Breakeven points
- Spread net premiums
- Spread max gain/loss

**Margin calculations you'll see:**
- Initial margin requirements
- SMA balances
- Buying power
- Maintenance margin calls
- Short margin equity

**Bond calculations you'll see:**
- Current yield
- Tax-equivalent yield
- Accrued interest

The formula sheet is your friend. Use the calculation drills until the formulas are automatic.
