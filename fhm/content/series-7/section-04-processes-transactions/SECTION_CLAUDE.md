## What This Section Covers

This section tests the back-end of securities transactions—what happens after a customer decides to buy or sell. It's about execution, confirmation, settlement, and documentation.

Topics include:
- Order types (market, limit, stop, stop-limit)
- Order entry and execution
- Trade confirmations
- Settlement dates and requirements
- Fails to deliver
- Customer account statements
- Record retention

### Why It Matters

While this is only 7% of the exam, these are skills you'll use daily. Understanding order types prevents costly errors. Knowing settlement rules keeps you compliant.

## Content Guidelines

### Voice & Tone

Follow FHM brand voice:
- Practical, procedural focus
- Use examples of order scenarios
- Explain consequences of errors
- One subtle humor moment per lesson

### Section-Specific Notes

- **Order types need clear examples**: "Stock at $50, limit order to buy at $48..."
- **Settlement timelines need specificity**: Exact dates and what happens when
- **Use mnemonics**: SLoBS and BLiSS for order placement
- **Include error scenarios**: What goes wrong and why

### Opening Hook Ideas

- The Knight Capital disaster ($440M lost in 45 minutes)
- History of settlement times (from T+5 to T+1)
- Why "good delivery" rules exist
- Famous trading errors and their consequences
