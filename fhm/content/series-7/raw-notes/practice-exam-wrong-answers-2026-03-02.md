### 1. SEC Creation
- **Q:** The SEC was created by the:
- **Your answer:** Securities Act of 1933 (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Securities Exchange Act of 1934
- **Key facts:** The 1933 Act was passed at the very end of 1933; the 1934 Act was passed at the very beginning of 1934. They were enacted "back-to-back." The 1934 Act created the SEC.

### 2. Exempt vs Non-Exempt Securities
- **Q:** All of the following are exempt from registration under the 1933 Act EXCEPT:
- **Your answer:** Insurance companies (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Investment companies
- **Key facts:** Exempt issuers: governments, agencies, municipals, insurance companies, banks. Investment company issues are NON-exempt — must be registered and sold with a prospectus under the 1933 Act.

### 3. Lead Underwriter Compensation
- **Q:** In a corporate underwriting, which is earned by the lead underwriter on each security sold?
- **Your answer:** Selling concession (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Management fee
- **Key facts:** The spread is gross compensation earned by the syndicate. Components: management fee (to syndicate manager, smallest portion), underwriting fee (to financially responsible member), selling concession (to whoever sells the share).

### 4. 403(b) Plan Eligibility
- **Q:** Which person would be least likely to contribute to a 403(b)?
- **Your answer:** Secretary at a foundation (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Student at a college
- **Key facts:** 403(b) plans are for employees of non-profit institutions (schools, universities, municipalities, hospitals). Students are NOT employees of the institution and do not qualify.

### 5. FINRA Employee Account Rules
- **Q:** Customer is an accounting supervisor at another FINRA firm. All procedures are required EXCEPT:
- **Your answer:** The associated person gives written notice to executing member (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Annual account summaries of all activity must be sent to employer member
- **Key facts:** Required: (1) prior written consent of employing member firm, (2) executing member notified in writing of employee's association, (3) on written request, executing member provides duplicate confirmations and statements. The rule applies to "associated persons" (officers, registered or unregistered employees), with exception for clerical/ministerial duties. Also applies to accounts at non-member institutions (banks, investment advisers).

### 6. Mutual Fund Redemption with Fee
- **Q:** Customer redeems 1,000 shares, NAV $9.20, POP $10.00, 1/2% redemption fee. Customer receives:
- **Your answer:** $9,200 (WRONG)
- **Correct:** $9,154
- **Key facts:** Redemption always at NAV. $9.20 x 1,000 = $9,200 gross. With 1/2% redemption fee: investor gets 99.5% of $9,200 = $9,154.

### 7. Basis Points and Bond Pricing
- **Q:** $10,000 municipal bond, 1 year to maturity, basis quote raised by 20 basis points. Price change:
- **Your answer:** $20.00 increase (WRONG)
- **Correct:** $20.00 decrease
- **Key facts:** 1 basis point = 0.01% = $0.10 per $1,000 par. 20 basis points = 0.20%. 0.002 x $10,000 = $20. Interest rates rise → bond price DECREASES. This question type only applies to bonds with 1 year to maturity (otherwise requires discounted cash flow, which is not tested).

### 8. Open Interest in Options
- **Q:** Which increases open interest?
- **Your answer:** Opening sale and closing purchase (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Opening purchase and opening sale
- **Key facts:** Opening transactions (both purchases AND sales) INCREASE open interest. Closing transactions (both purchases AND sales) DECREASE open interest. Greater open interest = greater likely trading volume.

### 9. Variable-Rate Demand Obligation (VRDO)
- **Q:** Security with floating rate, stated maturity, option to deliver to issuer for face value plus accrued interest:
- **Your answer:** Put option (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Variable-rate demand obligation (VRDO)
- **Key facts:** VRDO (also VRDN) = floating-rate obligation with long-term maturity. Interest resets daily/weekly/monthly. Includes a "put" feature back to the issuer at face value + accrued interest — but this is different from a standalone put option (which gives the right to sell at a strike price). Preferred stock = fixed dividends from a company. Bankers' acceptance = short-term credit instrument for business purchases, issued at a discount.

### 10. Interval Funds for Diversification
- **Q:** Wealthy investor, large public equity/fixed income portfolio, low liquidity needs, long horizon — most suitable:
- **Your answer:** Balanced mutual fund (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Interval fund
- **Key facts:** Investor already has public equities and fixed income — don't add more of the same. Interval funds: offer higher yields, allow share sales only at specific intervals (max 25% buyback), invest in illiquid assets (commercial real estate, private securities, below-investment grade debt), less correlated with public markets. Suitable for investors seeking higher income, tolerating low liquidity, with long time horizons.

### 11. RMD Penalty
- **Q:** Tax penalty for failing to withdraw RMD from a qualified retirement account:
- **Your answer:** 10% of the RMD (WRONG)
- **Correct:** 25% of the RMD
- **Key facts:** RMDs must begin the year the account holder turns 73. Penalty for failure = 25% of the RMD amount.

### 12. Smallest Component of Underwriting Spread (repeat of #3)
- **Q:** In a new issue underwriting, which is typically the smallest?
- **Your answer:** Selling concession (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Management fee
- **Key facts:** Same as #3. Spread = gross compensation. Management fee is the smallest portion (goes to syndicate manager). A syndicate member that sells a share for which it is financially responsible receives both the underwriting fee AND the selling concession.

### 13. IRA Transfers vs Rollovers
- **Q:** Which is TRUE about transfers of Individual Retirement Accounts?
- **Your answer:** A maximum of 1 transfer per year (WRONG)
- **Correct:** There is no limit on transfers
- **Key facts:** Transfers = trustee-to-trustee, unlimited per year. Rollovers = check made out to account holder, must be completed within 60 days, only 1 per year. The confusion: rollovers have the 1/year limit, transfers do not.

### 14. Loan Consent Agreement
- **Q:** What document permits a broker-dealer to borrow customer securities for short sales?
- **Your answer:** Hypothecation agreement (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Loan consent agreement
- **Key facts:** Hypothecation agreement = customer pledges securities as collateral for margin loan (mandatory for margin accounts). Loan consent agreement = customer permits firm to lend securities to short sellers (customary but NOT mandatory). Form 15g-2 = penny stock disclosure. Options agreement = for options accounts only.

### 15. Introducing Broker-Dealer
- **Q:** Which is FALSE about an introducing broker-dealer?
- **Your answer:** Must sign clearing agreement with a clearing broker-dealer (WRONG — this is TRUE)
- **Correct:** Cannot accept trades from customers (this is FALSE — they CAN accept trades)
- **Key facts:** Introducing BDs: sign clearing agreements with clearing firms, CAN accept trades, do NOT hold customer funds/securities, have lower net capital requirements. Clearing firm = "back office" (sends confirmations and statements). Customer complaints = introducing firm's responsibility. Rule also applies to accounts at non-member institutions (banks, investment advisers).

### 16. Outstanding Shares Calculation
- **Q:** 100,000 authorized, 20,000 issued, 5,000 treasury. Outstanding shares:
- **Your answer:** 120,000 (WRONG)
- **Correct:** 15,000
- **Key facts:** Outstanding = Issued - Treasury. 20,000 - 5,000 = 15,000. Authorized shares are the max allowed; they don't factor into the outstanding calculation.

### 17. Registrar's Role
- **Q:** The registrar is responsible for:
- **Your answer:** Recording changes of ownership (WRONG — that's the transfer agent)
- **Correct:** Overseeing/auditing the transfer agent
- **Key facts:** Registrar: oversees/audits the transfer agent, ensures outstanding and issued share counts are correct, ensures issued shares don't exceed authorized shares. Transfer agent: records ownership changes.

### 18. Bullish Leveraged Strategy
- **Q:** Wealthy, sophisticated, high-risk investor turns extremely bullish. Best recommendation:
- **Your answer:** Sell index puts (WRONG)
- **Correct:** Sell leveraged inverse ETFs
- **Key facts:** 300% leveraged inverse ETF based on S&P 500: if index rises 15%, ETF falls 3 x 15% = 45%. Short position on that ETF = large profit. If wrong, customer loses big. Inverse floaters = bonds with rate inversely tied to market rates (not tested, only a wrong-answer trap). Selling index calls = bearish. Selling index puts = moderately bullish but limited profit.

### 19. Municipal Bond Dollar Pricing
- **Q:** Customer purchases $5,000 of NY 3% G.O.'s at 90. Price excluding commissions/accrued interest:
- **Your answer:** $5,000 (WRONG)
- **Correct:** $4,500
- **Key facts:** Quoted at 90 = 90% of par value. 90% x $5,000 = $4,500. Municipal bonds quoted as dollar prices = "dollar bonds," usually term issues. Serial bonds are quoted on a yield-to-maturity basis.
