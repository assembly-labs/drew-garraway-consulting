# Section 1: Seeks Business for the Broker-Dealer

## Welcome

Before you can manage a portfolio, analyze a trade, or calculate a breakeven point, you need one thing: a client. This section covers how registered representatives build their book of business—and the rules that govern how they do it.

It's only 9% of your exam, but it's 100% of how your career starts. Every client relationship begins here.

---

## What You'll Learn

### Chapter 1: Prospecting Customers
- Types of customers (retail vs. institutional)
- Referral methods and best practices
- Cold calling rules and restrictions
- The difference between a lead and a prospect

### Chapter 2: Business Development
- Hosting investor seminars and presentations
- Advertising and sales literature requirements
- Social media compliance
- Networking strategies that work (and stay compliant)

### Chapter 3: Compliance in Prospecting
- Do-Not-Call rules (who you can't call)
- Record-keeping requirements
- Telemarketing regulations
- State registration considerations

## Study Approach

This section is rule-heavy but straightforward. Focus on:

1. **Know the exceptions**: Do-Not-Call rules have specific exceptions (existing customers, business relationships)
2. **Understand approval requirements**: Which communications need pre-approval vs. post-review
3. **Remember timeframes**: Record retention periods, registration requirements

**Time allocation:** 5-8 hours total
