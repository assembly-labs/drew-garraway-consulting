## What This Section Covers

This section tests your understanding of what happens when a customer decides to open an account. It's about gathering information, assessing suitability, and setting up accounts properly.

Topics include:
- Types of accounts (individual, joint, corporate, fiduciary)
- Know Your Customer (KYC) requirements
- Financial profile assessment
- Investment objectives and risk tolerance
- Suitability obligations (FINRA Rule 2111)
- Account documentation and agreements

### Why It Matters

Suitability violations are among the most common compliance failures in the industry. Understanding how to properly evaluate customers and document their profiles protects both the client and the firm.

## Chapters in This Section

1. **Chapter 1: Account Types**
   - Individual accounts
   - Joint accounts (JTWROS, TIC, community property)
   - Corporate and partnership accounts
   - Trust and estate accounts
   - Custodial accounts (UGMA/UTMA)

2. **Chapter 2: Customer Profile**
   - KYC requirements (FINRA Rule 2090)
   - Financial profile elements
   - Investment objectives
   - Risk tolerance assessment
   - Time horizon and liquidity needs

3. **Chapter 3: Suitability**
   - Suitability obligations (FINRA Rule 2111)
   - Three suitability obligations (reasonable-basis, customer-specific, quantitative)
   - Documentation requirements
   - Suitability for specific products

4. **Chapter 4: Account Documentation**
   - New account forms
   - Customer agreements
   - Margin agreements (hypothecation, credit disclosure, loan consent)
   - Options agreements and approval levels
