# Series 7 - Introduction

## Exam at a Glance

The **Series 7 General Securities Representative Exam** is administered by the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority (FINRA).

### Exam Format
- **125 multiple-choice questions** + 5 "trial" questions = **130 total questions**
- **Time limit:** 3 hours 45 minutes
- Trial questions are inserted randomly by FINRA to validate new questions and **do not count** in grading
- **Prerequisite:** Securities Industry Essentials (SIE) Exam must be completed for Series 7 license

### Passing Score
- **Passing grade: 72%**
- Actual grading uses a statistical adjustment process called "equating"
- Equating scores to a common scale accounts for slight variations in difficulty between exam sets
- Ensures fair comparison and same passing standard regardless of which exam items received

### Exam Requirements
- Must be **sponsored by a FINRA member firm**
- Complete a **U4 application**
- Be **fingerprinted**
- Complete a **background check**

### Retake Policy
- **Failed once:** Wait 30 days to retake
- **Failed twice:** Wait 30 days to retake
- **Failed 3+ times:** Wait **180 days** between each successive retake attempt

## How to Use the Program

The Pass Perfect learning model employs a **building-block approach**:

### Study Structure
1. **Complete each section** → Take a "Check Your Understanding" quiz
2. **Complete all sections in a chapter** → Take a Chapter Test
3. **Complete several chapters** → Take a Mastery Exam (cumulative review)
4. **Complete entire course** → Take Final Exams

### Program Features
- Reinforces key concepts and objectives
- Measures how much you know
- Identifies areas of weakness
- **Unlimited retakes** to build confidence in test taking
- Practice timing and pacing for each question
- Practice as much as needed to achieve competency

### Recommended Videos
- **"Exam Prep Mistakes"** - Avoid common preparation errors
- **"Loopholes to Avoid on Test Day"** - Tips for test day success

> **Tip:** Come back to watch the "Loopholes" video again before your FINRA exam to reinforce the concepts.
