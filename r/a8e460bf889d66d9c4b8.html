Vendor Onboarding

How a new vendor joins Berwyn

Your 15-step process confirmed we need a real vendor management system, not just a contact list. For now, we're storing all the details you currently keep in your head — vendor category, availability, stall preferences, power/access needs, season dates.

A full vendor portal where they can submit their own documents and manage their own profile is coming in a later version.

Vendor Rotation

Managing 70+ vendors with 25-30 selling weekly

Your rotation logic — core vendors every week, similar vendors alternate, fill genre gaps — is too nuanced to automate right now. But every vendor will have a category tag and season dates, so you can quickly filter and sort when planning each week. The weekly reset tool (more on that below) makes toggling vendors on and off fast.

Full automated scheduling with conflict detection is planned for a later version.

Stall Locations

How you assign stall spots

We're storing stall preferences and special needs (power, flat ground, van access) on every vendor profile now. A visual drag-and-drop market map is coming in a later version.

Vendor Compliance

Tracking insurance, licenses, certifications

"This is a huge pain. This was my job and it's since been delegated to Erika which I'm not sure she keeps up with weekly/monthly."

We hear you. This is planned for after launch, but given how painful it is, we're considering pulling a basic version forward — expiration date tracking with automatic alerts when documents are about to lapse.

Your ideal — vendors submit their own documents, everyone gets notified if something lapses — is exactly what we'll build.

Fee Structure

How vendors pay you

For version 1, you'll continue collecting vendor fees the way you do now. Digital invoicing and payment tracking is coming in a later version — including the no-show fee you mentioned wanting to enforce.

Vendor Payments

Cash/check collection and late payments

Noted your experience with monthly invoicing not working — any future digital payment system will be structured around market-day collection, not monthly billing.

Vendor Communication

How you talk to vendors (email, text, phone)

The biggest improvement here: after orders close each week, Gather will automatically create vendor order sheets showing exactly what each vendor needs to bring. You can email those directly from the dashboard — no more manually tallying quantities.

A vendor portal where they can view their orders and confirm availability is coming in a later version.

Weather & Cancellations

How you handle last-minute changes

Since you rarely cancel (maybe twice in three seasons), we don't need anything special for this in version 1. Your existing email/text approach works fine for the rare weather event.

Customer Texting

What messages you send each week

"Tuesday morning I would manually text our shoppers to remind them that the market closes at noon and to get their order in. I usually included something like 'new milk jawn flavor' or some other highlight to catch their attention."

"Market is open" — Text + email when the ordering window opens. You can add a weekly highlight (like the milk jawn).

"Last chance to order" — Text + email a few hours before ordering closes.

Messages will feel like they come from you and Carlo — not a robot. Customers can still text back and reach a real person.

Order-to-Pickup Process

Your full 20-step workflow

This was the single most valuable answer. It revealed several steps we were completely missing. Here's how Gather handles the key moments:

After ordering closes — Vendor order sheets are generated automatically. No more manual tallying.

Printing packing slips — One slip per customer, already sorted by their pickup time. Items on each slip are organized by where they're stored (cold, dry, produce, fragile). Checkboxes for your assembly team. A tear-off bag label. Hit print and go.

Assembly morning — First-time customers are flagged so you can double-check their orders. Items like flowers, ice cream, and delicate pastries are marked "grab at pickup" so your runner knows not to pre-bag them.

Pickup — Customers check in (scan or name lookup), runner grabs their bags plus any fresh items.

After pickup — Credits and comps are logged digitally (no more clipboard). First-time customers get an automatic thank-you email.

Prep for next week — Collections get updated, products get toggled on/off with one click instead of rebuilding the whole site.

Print Materials

What you print each week and who uses it

You described six types. Here's how each one works in Gather:

Vendor order sheets — Generated automatically after ordering closes. Printable or emailable.

Master inventory list — Available on the dashboard and printable.

Customer packing slips — One per customer, sorted by time slot and pick zone, with checkboxes and a bag label. Batch-printable.

Pickups per time slot — Dashboard view, grouped by time.

Bag name labels — Built into the packing slip as a tear-off. No more hand-writing Post-its.

Items owed clipboard — Digital. When a vendor shorts you, log it. It carries forward to the customer's next order automatically.

"We had a running clipboard of items owed to customers (like if a vendor shorted us on a delivery and we couldn't make it right by pickup time, we would add it to their next order)."
