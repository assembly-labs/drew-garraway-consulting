Why these features were added

Quick context on the six themes and why they're MVP, not Phase 2:

  • Packing slips with pick zone sorting — Dara's #1 automation request. Without it, they'll print our lists and manually re-sort them, which is what they already do. This is the core fulfillment workflow.
  • Vendor purchase orders — How vendors know what to prepare. Currently done manually after ordering closes. Different from pick lists (pick lists = packing bags, POs = ordering from vendors). Critical path.
  • Automated customer messaging — Dara's #2 pain point. She manually texts hundreds of people on Tuesdays. Without automation, Gather adds work instead of removing it.
  • Collections system — Revenue driver. Dara says customers spent more because of curation (Carlo's Favorites, Award Winners, Limited Run). This is the business model differentiator vs. generic grocery.
  • Customer credits / items owed — When a vendor shorts a delivery, they credit the customer for next week. Currently tracked on a clipboard. P1 but important for recurring customer relationships.
  • Weekly product reset — Dara's #1 pain point overall. She rebuilds the entire product listing every week. Bulk tools save hours of manual work.

Technical notes for review

  • Twilio SMS is the biggest new integration. O68 is the heavy lift (Twilio setup, template system, scheduling); O69-O70 reuse the same infra and are incremental.
  • Customer entity is new to the data model. v1.0 treated customers as anonymous (email on order). v1.2 needs a Customer record to track order history, notes, tags, SMS prefs, and credits. This is foundational work that enables O68-O75.
  • Collection system is standard CRUD + join table. The drag-to-reorder in A40 and carousel in C57 are the most design-heavy parts.
  • Packing slip print CSS (O64) needs real-printer testing. Same as pick lists — budget time for print formatting.
  • PO generator (O66) queries are essentially the inverse of pick lists — aggregate by vendor instead of by customer. The email-to-vendor capability reuses Resend infra.
  • Scheduled jobs for automated messages need a reliable trigger. Vercel Cron Jobs or a lightweight queue. This is infra that didn't exist in v1.0.
Key architectural question (unchanged from v1.0): The Berwyn model is wholesale-resale (Gather/CH buys from vendors via POs, resells to customers), not marketplace pass-through. This may simplify the Stripe implementation (single merchant account, no Connect needed for MVP). Worth discussing — could significantly reduce payments complexity if confirmed.
