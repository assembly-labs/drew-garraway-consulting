Article 4: Architectural Standards and Modifications

Section 4.1 - Architectural Review Committee Authority

The Architectural Review Committee ("ARC") shall have exclusive jurisdiction over all exterior modifications, additions, or alterations to any Lot or the exterior of any structure within the Community. No Owner shall make any exterior modification without first obtaining written approval from the ARC.

The ARC shall consist of three (3) to five (5) members appointed by the Board of Directors. The ARC shall establish and publish guidelines for architectural standards, which may be amended from time to time.

Section 4.2 - Fences and Walls

The construction, installation, or modification of any fence, wall, or similar boundary structure shall require prior written approval from the ARC. The following standards shall apply to all fence installations:

  • Maximum Height: Six (6) feet for rear and side yard fences; four (4) feet for front yard fences
  • Approved Materials: Wood (cedar, redwood, or pressure-treated pine) or vinyl in approved colors. Chain link, wire mesh, and metal fencing are prohibited in all areas visible from streets or common areas.
  • Color Requirements: All fences must be finished in earth-tone colors from the approved palette, including: Natural wood stain, White, Tan, Brown, or Gray.
  • Setback Requirements: All fences must be set back a minimum of three (3) feet from any property line shared with common areas or neighboring lots.

Application Process: Owners must submit an ARC Application Form along with: (a) a site plan showing proposed fence location, (b) material specifications and color samples, (c) the $50.00 non-refundable application fee. The ARC shall respond within thirty (30) days of receipt of a complete application.

Important Notice: Construction shall not commence until written approval is received. Unapproved modifications may be subject to fines and/or mandatory removal at Owner's expense.

Article 6: Vehicle and Parking Regulations

Section 6.4 - Prohibited Vehicles

The following vehicles may not be parked, stored, or maintained on any Lot, driveway, street, or common area within the Community, except as specifically provided herein:

  • Recreational Vehicles: Including but not limited to motorhomes, campers, travel trailers, fifth-wheel trailers, and similar vehicles designed for temporary living accommodations.
  • Boats and Watercraft: Including boats, jet skis, kayaks (when on trailers), and boat trailers of any kind.
  • Trailers: Including utility trailers, enclosed trailers, flatbed trailers, and similar conveyances, whether attached to a vehicle or not.
  • Commercial Vehicles: Vehicles displaying commercial signage, lettering, or logos; vehicles with mounted equipment (ladders, pipe racks, etc.); and vehicles exceeding one (1) ton rated capacity.
  • Inoperable Vehicles: Any vehicle that is not currently registered, does not display valid license plates, or is not in operating condition.

Temporary Exception: Recreational vehicles and trailers may be parked in driveways for a period not to exceed forty-eight (48) hours for the purpose of loading, unloading, or preparation for travel, provided that written notice is given to the management office at least 24 hours in advance.

Purpose: These restrictions are intended to maintain the residential character and aesthetic appearance of the Community, preserve property values, and ensure adequate parking availability for residents and guests.

Article 7: Pet and Animal Policies

Section 7.1 - Permitted Animals

Owners and residents may keep common domestic household pets, including dogs, cats, birds (non-poultry), and fish, subject to the restrictions and requirements set forth in this Article. The keeping of pets is a privilege, not a right, and may be revoked for violations of these policies.

Maximum Number: No more than two (2) pets per household shall be permitted, excluding fish maintained in aquariums of 50 gallons or less.

Section 7.2 - Dog Restrictions

Dogs are permitted subject to the following restrictions:

  • Weight Limit: Dogs shall not exceed fifty (50) pounds at maturity. Owners of dogs exceeding this weight limit as of the effective date of these CC&Rs may keep such dogs but may not replace them with dogs exceeding the weight limit.
  • Breed Restrictions: The following breeds, and any mix thereof, are prohibited: Pit Bull Terriers (American Pit Bull Terrier, American Staffordshire Terrier, Staffordshire Bull Terrier), Rottweilers, and Wolf Hybrids.
  • Leash Requirement: All dogs must be kept on a leash not exceeding six (6) feet in length when outside the Owner's Lot.
  • Waste Removal: Owners must immediately remove and properly dispose of pet waste.

Section 7.3 - Registration and Fees

All pets must be registered with the Association within fourteen (14) days of acquisition or move-in. Registration requires:

  • Completed Pet Registration Form
  • Proof of current vaccinations (rabies, distemper for dogs; rabies for cats)
  • Proof of spay/neuter (or signed waiver with additional deposit)
  • Current photograph of the pet

Pet Deposit: A refundable pet deposit of Three Hundred Dollars ($300.00) per pet is required, payable upon registration.

Monthly Pet Rent: A non-refundable monthly pet fee of Twenty-Five Dollars ($25.00) per pet shall be added to the Owner's regular assessment.

Article 8: Leasing and Rental Restrictions

Section 8.2 - Minimum Lease Term

Short-Term Rentals Prohibited: No Lot or dwelling unit may be leased or rented for a period of less than thirty (30) consecutive days. Daily, weekly, and other short-term rentals, including but not limited to rentals through platforms such as Airbnb, VRBO, HomeAway, or similar services, are strictly prohibited.

This restriction applies regardless of whether the Owner is present during the rental period. "House-sitting" arrangements where compensation is exchanged shall be considered rentals subject to this restriction.

Section 8.3 - Notification Requirements

Prior to leasing any Lot or dwelling unit, the Owner must:

  1. Submit a completed Rental Notification Form to the Association at least fourteen (14) days before the lease commencement date.
  2. Provide the tenant's name, contact information, and emergency contact.
  3. Provide a copy of the executed lease agreement.
  4. Ensure the tenant receives a copy of the CC&Rs, Bylaws, and Community Rules.
  5. Obtain tenant's signed acknowledgment of receipt of governing documents.

Section 8.4 - Owner Responsibility

The Owner shall remain fully responsible for compliance with all Association governing documents, including the actions of their tenants, guests, and invitees. Violations by tenants shall be treated as violations by the Owner and may result in fines and other enforcement actions against the Owner.

Repeated violations by a tenant may result in the Board requiring termination of the lease as a condition of avoiding further enforcement action.
