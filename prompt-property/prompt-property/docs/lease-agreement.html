Section 10: Maintenance and Repairs

10.1 Tenant shall maintain the Premises in a clean and sanitary condition and shall not permit any waste or damage to the Premises beyond normal wear and tear.

10.2 Tenant shall promptly notify Landlord in writing of any maintenance issues, defects, or needed repairs.

10.3 Landlord shall be responsible for repairs to structural elements, plumbing systems, electrical systems, and appliances provided by Landlord, except where damage is caused by Tenant's negligence or misuse.

Section 11: Entry and Inspection

11.1 Landlord may enter the Premises with at least 24 hours written notice for inspections, repairs, or to show the unit to prospective tenants or buyers.

11.2 Landlord may enter without notice in case of emergency.

Section 12: Move-Out Procedures

12.1 - Notice of Intent to Vacate

Required Notice Period: Tenant must provide written notice of intent to vacate at least thirty (30) days prior to the intended move-out date. Notice shall be delivered via the resident portal, email to management, or certified mail.

Failure to provide proper notice may result in forfeiture of security deposit or liability for additional rent.

12.2 - Scheduling Move-Out

To ensure a smooth move-out process and minimize disruption to other residents:

  • Elevator Reservation: Tenant must reserve the service elevator at least seventy-two (72) hours in advance of the scheduled move.
  • Permitted Hours: Moving activities are permitted Monday through Saturday, 8:00 AM to 6:00 PM. No moving activities are permitted on Sundays or observed holidays.
  • Moving Deposit: A refundable moving deposit of Fifty Dollars ($50.00) is required to reserve the elevator and common area access.

12.3 - Condition of Premises at Move-Out

Tenant shall return the Premises in the same condition as received, reasonable wear and tear excepted. Prior to move-out, Tenant shall:

  • Remove all personal belongings and debris
  • Clean all rooms, appliances, fixtures, and surfaces thoroughly
  • Patch small nail holes (1/4" or smaller) with spackling compound
  • Replace any burned-out light bulbs
  • Remove all trash and dispose properly
  • Clean interior of all cabinets, closets, and storage areas
  • Defrost and clean refrigerator/freezer
  • Clean oven, stovetop, and range hood
  • Clean bathrooms including toilet, tub/shower, and sink
  • Vacuum carpets and mop hard floors
  • Clean windows (interior)

12.4 - Return of Keys and Access Devices

All Keys Must Be Returned: Tenant shall return all keys, key fobs, access cards, parking passes, mailbox keys, and any other access devices to the management office by 12:00 PM (noon) on the move-out date.

Unreturned keys or access devices will result in a charge for re-keying or replacement, deducted from the security deposit.

12.5 - Move-Out Inspection

Tenant has the right to request a pre-move-out inspection to identify any issues that may result in security deposit deductions. Tenant may also be present during the final move-out inspection.

Section 13: Security Deposit

13.1 - Return of Security Deposit

Timeline: Landlord shall return the security deposit, less any lawful deductions, within twenty-one (21) days after Tenant has vacated the Premises and returned all keys.

If deductions are made, Landlord shall provide an itemized statement of deductions along with any remaining balance.

13.2 - Permissible Deductions

Landlord may deduct from the security deposit for:

  • Unpaid rent or other charges due under the lease
  • Repair of damages beyond normal wear and tear
  • Cleaning costs if unit is not returned in clean condition
  • Replacement of unreturned keys or access devices
  • Costs to remove abandoned personal property

Normal Wear and Tear: Landlord shall not deduct for normal wear and tear, which includes minor scuffs on walls, worn carpet in traffic areas, faded paint, or minor marks that can be cleaned.
