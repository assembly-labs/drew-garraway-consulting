FAQ
Do I need an iPhone?
Yes. TOMO is distributed through Apple TestFlight, which requires an iPhone running iOS 16 or later. Android is not supported during the beta.
Is it really free?
Completely free during the beta. When TOMO launches publicly it will be a paid subscription, but beta testers from Alliance Paoli will get a heavily discounted rate as a thank-you.
How long does the beta last?
There's no hard end date. The beta runs until we're confident the app is ready for a public launch. You'll have plenty of notice before anything changes.
What happens to my data if the beta ends?
Your training data carries over. When TOMO launches publicly, your account and all your logged sessions will be there waiting for you. Nothing gets lost.
Is my data private?
Yes. Your training data is yours. Nobody else at the gym can see your sessions, notes, or stats. Not your coach, not your training partners. Drew can access anonymized usage data to improve the app, but your personal journal is private.
How much time does it take after each class?
About 90 seconds. You talk through your session naturally (what you drilled, how rolls went) and TOMO handles the rest. It's designed for when you're exhausted and just want to get to your car.
Do I have to log every single session?
No pressure. Log what you can. The app gets more useful the more you use it, but there's no minimum requirement. Even a few sessions a week gives you a solid picture of your training.
What if I forget to log right after class?
You can always log later. The app doesn't lock you out. But the voice feature works best when your session is still fresh, so we recommend doing it in the parking lot or on your drive home.
What does "beta" actually mean? Will it crash on me?
It means the app is still being actively developed. It's stable and usable, but you might occasionally hit a bug or see something that looks rough around the edges. That's exactly why we need testers, to find those things before the public launch.
How do I report bugs or give feedback?
Email drew@assemblylabs.co or grab Drew at the gym. A quick text or voice note about what happened is perfect. You don't need to write a formal report.
Does it work without Wi-Fi?
You'll need an internet connection (Wi-Fi or cellular) to log sessions since the voice processing happens server-side.
I'm a white belt. Is this useful for me?
Especially for you. White belts are learning the most new material the fastest. TOMO helps you remember what you actually drilled instead of losing it by the next class. The app personalizes its experience based on your belt level.
I'm a blue belt. Is this useful for me?
Blue belt is where most people quit. The ones who don't are the ones who train with intention. TOMO helps you see patterns in your training, track what you're actually working on, and stay consistent through the plateau. The features coming later will help you refine your game even further and build a clear path toward purple.
I'm a purple or brown belt. Is this still useful?
At your level, the details matter more than ever. You're refining systems, closing gaps, and sharpening what already works. TOMO gives you a structured record to review and spot trends you might miss session to session. The features we're building next are specifically designed for higher belts looking to tighten their game and accelerate toward black belt.
Can I share the app with friends at other gyms?
Not right now. This beta is just for Alliance Paoli members. If you know someone who'd be a good fit, send them my way and I'll get them set up.
What do you do with my voice recordings?
Voice recordings are used to transcribe your session into text, then the original audio is deleted. We don't store your voice permanently. Only the written transcript that becomes your journal entry.
Can my kid use this?
Not yet. We're building a feature that will let parents track their kid's progress, but we need to get the adult experience right first to make sure everything is safe and solid. Privacy and data protection for minors is something we take very seriously. It's coming.
