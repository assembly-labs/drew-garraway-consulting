Broken Things (Must Fix Before Launch)

3
BUG-001 After saving a session, the app starts a new recording instead of going back to Journal
Blocker Quick fix Session Logger

What's happening

You record a session, review it, tap "Save Session." Instead of seeing the success message and landing on your Journal, the app immediately throws you into a new recording. You're stuck in a loop.

Why it's happening

The app has two things fighting each other. One shows the "Session logged" success screen for 5 seconds, then sends you to Journal. The other watches for the Log tab to become active and resets the form. That second one fires too early, before the success screen even shows, and drops you into a fresh recording.

The fix

Make the form reset only happen when you leave the Log tab and come back, not while you're still on it watching the success screen.

BUG-002 Keyboard covers the stripe picker during onboarding
High priority Quick fix Onboarding: About You

What's happening

You type your name, the keyboard pops up. You tap your belt color. The stripe picker appears below the belt row, but the keyboard is still open and blocking it. Most users will tap "Continue" without ever seeing the stripe options, so everyone gets saved as 0 stripes.

The fix

When you tap a belt color, the keyboard automatically closes and the screen scrolls down to reveal the stripe picker. Two small changes, instant improvement.

BUG-003 App skips onboarding if a previous account was deleted
High priority Quick fix Login / Startup

What's happening

If you delete your account (or it gets wiped) and sign up again, the app skips onboarding entirely and drops you into the main app with no profile, no name, no gym, nothing. The profile page is blank. Everything that depends on your profile breaks.

Why it's happening

The app saves a "this person finished onboarding" note on your phone. When the account gets wiped on the server, that note is still on your phone. So the app thinks you already onboarded, even though your profile no longer exists.

The fix

When the app checks for your profile and finds nothing, clear that old note and send you through onboarding again.

User Experience Fixes

6

Things that work but could work better for new users.

UX-001 Remove the "NEAR YOU" label from the gym picker
Nice to have Quick fix Onboarding: Your Training

What's wrong

You just tapped "Enable Location." Of course the gyms are near you. The label is stating the obvious. Each gym card already shows the distance in miles.

The fix

Remove the "NEAR YOU" label. The gym cards speak for themselves.

UX-002 Make it clear that typing your gym name will search the database
Nice to have Quick fix Onboarding: Your Training

What's wrong

Below the nearby gyms it says "Not here? Type your gym name." That sounds like you're manually typing it in. But there's actually a full search with auto-suggestions built in. Users who aren't near their gym don't realize it will help them find it.

The fix

Replace the hint text with a visible search box that says "Search for your gym." As they type, suggestions appear. If their gym isn't in the database, the app accepts whatever they typed as a new gym.

UX-003 Add "Other" to Training Goals
Nice to have 1 minute Onboarding: Your Training

What's wrong

The options are Competition, Fitness, Hobby, and Mental Health. Some people train for self-defense, community, or reasons that don't fit these four.

The fix

Add an "Other" option to the list.

UX-004 Replace subjective experience labels with actual time training
Nice to have Quick fix Onboarding: Your Training

What's wrong

The options say "New," "Beginner," "Intermediate," "Experienced." These are subjective. A 2-year white belt might call themselves either "beginner" or "intermediate." We're not getting useful data.

The fix

Rename the section to "Total Years of Training" and change the options to just the time ranges: "< 6 months", "6mo - 2yr", "2 - 5yr", "5+ yr". Objective, no judgment.

UX-005 Teach users what to say during the onboarding chat
Important Medium effort Onboarding: Get Started

What's wrong

The onboarding chat says "After each session, just talk." But it never explains what to talk about. Users finish onboarding and have no idea what to say when they hit record. "Just talk" is vague.

The fix

Add 1-2 more chat bubbles that coach the user:

"Tell me how long you trained, what techniques you drilled, and how your rounds went. The more detail, the better your journal."

Then show an example of what good input sounds like:

"Like: Trained gi today for about an hour. Worked on half guard sweeps. Hit two in live rolls but got caught in a triangle."

This way, the very first time they record, they know exactly what to say.

UX-006 Keep Journal empty state simple (no tutorial page)
Already done No work needed Journal

Context

We tried adding a detailed tutorial page to the Journal for first-time users. It caused problems and was reverted. The coaching content belongs in the onboarding chat (UX-005 above), not on the Journal page. This is just a note to confirm we keep the simple "No Sessions Yet" screen.

Look and Feel Upgrades

14

Design improvements to make TOMO feel like a product built by designers, not generated by a tool. Sorted by impact.

DA-018 Everything looks the same: cards, inputs, chips all use identical styling
High impact Big lift

What's wrong

Every single surface in the app uses the exact same dark gray background with the same border. A session card looks identical to a text input looks identical to a filter chip. There's no visual depth. Your eye can't tell what's content vs. what's a button vs. what's a container. This is the single biggest thing that makes an app look template-generated.

The fix

Create 3 levels of visual depth. Content cards (like session entries) get a slightly different shade with subtle shadows. Buttons and inputs keep the current look. Background containers go even darker. This creates layers, like how a well-designed room has foreground, midground, and background.

DA-019 Gold is used on everything, so it means nothing
High impact Big lift

What's wrong

Gold appears on about 30 different elements: buttons, selected chips, dots, tab icons, borders, spinners, badges. When your signature color is on everything, it stops being a signature. It just becomes noise. Think of how a luxury brand uses its accent color on maybe 2-3 things per page. That's what makes it pop.

The fix

Save gold for moments that matter: the main action button on each screen, the TOMO logo, and key brand moments. Everything else that's "selected" or "active" should use a subtle white highlight instead. This way, when something IS gold, your eye immediately knows "this is important."

DA-020 Every screen has the same exact header, no personality
Medium impact Medium effort

What's wrong

"Journal." "Insights." "Profile." All in the same size, same weight, same position. It looks like someone typed different words into the same template. Each screen should feel like it was designed on purpose.

The fix

Give each screen its own typographic personality. The Journal could drop its title (the tab already says "Journal"). Insights could add a subtitle. Onboarding titles could highlight the first word in gold. Small differences that signal intentional design.

DA-021 The bottom tab bar looks like every other app
Medium impact Medium effort

What's wrong

The tab bar at the bottom is the most-seen element in the entire app, and it's completely stock. Icons, labels, a line at the top. Every dark-mode app template ships with this exact bar.

The fix

Remove the text labels (the icons are clear enough with only 3 tabs). Add a small gold dot under the active tab. Give the center gold "+" button more depth. These are small touches, but the tab bar is seen on every single screen, so the impact is huge.

DA-022 Profile page looks like a generic settings screen
Medium impact Big lift

What's wrong

Big centered circle at the top, name below it, then a list of settings rows. This is the default profile page of every app since 2015. For a BJJ training journal, this page should feel like looking at your fighter card, not your account settings.

The fix

Redesign as a left-aligned "fighter card." Name big and bold, belt shown as a visual bar, gym name below. Add a stats strip: total sessions logged, sessions this week, member since. Settings rows move below the stats. The profile becomes about your training identity, not account management.

DA-023 Session cards in the Journal all look identical
Medium impact Quick fix

What's wrong

A 2-hour competition prep with 6 rounds of sparring looks exactly the same as a 30-minute light drilling session. Every card is the same gray box. Your training journal should develop visual texture over time, not look like a spreadsheet.

The fix

Add a colored stripe on the left edge of each card: blue for Gi sessions, orange for No-Gi. This one small change transforms the Journal from "list of gray boxes" into a color-coded training history you can scan at a glance.

DA-024 The monospace font is used for two different purposes without clear rules
Polish Quick fix

What's wrong

JetBrains Mono (the monospace font) is used for tiny uppercase labels AND for regular-sized data values. These look very different but there's no formal rule about when to use which. It's subtle, but it contributes to a feeling of "not quite intentional."

The fix

Define two clear roles: "label" style (small, uppercase, letter-spaced) and "data" style (normal size, bold). Once the rules exist, applying them consistently makes the whole app feel more considered.

DA-025 Onboarding progress bar is nearly invisible
Polish Quick fix

What's wrong

The progress bar at the top of onboarding screens is 3 pixels tall and the track is almost the same shade as the background. You can see the gold fill, but you can't tell how far along you are because the track is invisible.

The fix

Make it slightly thicker (4px) and make the track more visible. Add small dot markers at each step (25%, 50%, 75%, 100%) so users feel momentum through the flow.

DA-026 Login screen looks like a template
Polish Quick fix

What's wrong

The TOMO logo is centered with "BJJ TRAINING JOURNAL" underneath. It looks like every login screen template. The Welcome screen (with the typewriter effect) has real personality. The login screen has none.

The fix

Left-align the logo (editorial apps like Linear do this and it immediately looks more premium). Remove the "BJJ TRAINING JOURNAL" text (by the time someone is signing in, they know what the app is). Less is more here.

DA-027 Everything is spaced exactly the same, creating a monotone rhythm
Polish Medium effort

What's wrong

Nearly every gap between sections is the same size (24px). It's like reading a page where every paragraph has the same spacing. Your eye moves down at a constant pace with no visual "breathing room" between major sections. Designed apps vary their spacing to create rhythm and emphasis.

The fix

Tighter spacing within related items (like a label and its input). Medium spacing between sibling elements. Generous breathing room before big section changes. The variety creates hierarchy without any other visual changes.

DA-028 Selectable chips have inconsistent shapes across screens
Polish Quick fix

What's wrong

The filter pills on the Journal screen are fully rounded (pill shape). The chips in the session logger are slightly rounded rectangles. They do the same thing but look different. A designed system has consistent rules.

The fix

Standardize: all selectable options use slightly rounded rectangles. Save the fully rounded "pill" shape for status badges (like the Gi/No-Gi label on session cards).

DA-029 The recording screen looks like every other voice app
Medium impact Medium-big lift

What's wrong

Pulsing gold circle with a microphone icon and a small timer. This is the default recording UI of every voice app ever made. But this screen is TOMO's signature moment. Every user sees it every single session. If it looks generic, the whole brand feels generic.

The fix

Replace the mic icon with the TOMO kanji character inside a gold ring. Make the timer massive and bold (it's the most important info during recording). Add subtle audio level bars that move with your voice so you can see the app is actually "hearing" you. This should be the screen that makes someone say "who designed this?"

DA-030 Belt picker uses generic colored dots instead of belt-shaped bars
Polish Quick fix

What's wrong

During onboarding, you pick your belt by tapping colored circles. White circle, blue circle, purple circle. They look like dots in a color picker, not like BJJ belts. Every BJJ app does this because it's the easy default.

The fix

Change the circles to horizontal bars that actually look like belts. It's a small detail that any practitioner would immediately recognize and appreciate. The kind of thing that signals "someone who trains built this."

DA-031 Success screen disappears before you can read it
Polish Quick fix

What's wrong

After you save a session, there's a nice "Session logged" screen with a personalized message. But it auto-disappears after 5 seconds. Some people want to read the message. Others want to close the app immediately. Neither group has control.

The fix

Add a "Done" button so you can leave immediately if you want. Keep the 5-second auto-dismiss for people who just set their phone down. Best of both worlds.
