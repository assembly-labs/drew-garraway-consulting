Design Decisions
  • 1Module placement: The Belt & Stripe History lives below the stats grid on the Profile page. It is not a separate page -- it is contextual information about the practitioner, not a standalone workflow.
  • 2Avatar border color: The avatar ring border matches the user's current belt color, reinforcing identity at a glance without requiring text.
  • 3Vertical timeline: A simple top-to-bottom timeline with color-coded dots gives an immediate visual sense of progression. Each belt color renders as its actual color for instant recognition.
  • 4Time at belt: Displayed in monospace to signal that it is a computed value. This gives the user passive awareness of their tenure without gamifying it.
  • 5"Started Training" anchor: A gold star dot marks the origin point, grounding the entire timeline. This is not a belt event but a personal milestone.
  • 6Disclaimer text: Italicized and de-emphasized, but always present. Belt promotions belong to the coach. This module only records what has already happened, for the practitioner's own reference.
  • 7Gold outline button: "Record a Promotion" uses an outline style rather than a filled button to signal that this is an infrequent action, not a primary workflow.

Design Decisions
  • 1Bottom sheet pattern: The promotion form appears as a bottom sheet modal rather than navigating to a new page. This keeps the user grounded in the Profile context and signals that this is a supplementary action.
  • 2Belt circles: Visual belt selection using actual belt colors. The selected belt gets a gold border and subtle glow. This is faster and more intuitive than a dropdown.
  • 3Stripe selector: Simple 0-4 toggle buttons. These map directly to the physical stripes on a belt. No abstraction needed -- practitioners already think in these terms.
  • 4Past-date restriction: The date picker is limited to past dates only. Promotions are recorded after they happen, never in advance. The helper text makes this explicit.
  • 5Optional notes field: Allows practitioners to capture context ("Promoted by Professor Silva at open mat") without requiring it. Keeps the flow fast for those who just want to record the fact.
  • 6Permanence warning: "You can add corrections but not delete entries" establishes trust and seriousness. This is a journal -- you correct forward, you do not erase history.
  • 7Gold "Save" button: Filled gold for the primary action, muted gray for cancel. The visual weight makes it clear which action is constructive.
  • 8No validation yet: The Save button is always enabled in this mockup. In implementation, it would be disabled until both belt and date are selected.

Design Decisions
  • 1Immediate profile update: After recording a purple belt promotion, the entire profile reflects the change -- avatar border becomes purple, rank text updates, and the belt badge changes. The system should feel cohesive, not fragmented.
  • 2Celebration glow: The newest timeline entry gets a subtle purple glow on its dot and a "NEW" badge. This is a brief moment of recognition without being over-the-top. No confetti, no animations -- just a quiet acknowledgment.
  • 3"Just started!" text: The time-at-belt text changes to green and reads "Just started!" for a newly recorded belt. This gives a positive, forward-looking tone without gamifying the experience.
  • 4Gap filling: Notice that Blue Belt 3 Stripes is missing from the timeline. That is fine. Practitioners do not always record every stripe. The timeline shows what was recorded, not what "should" exist. No judgment, no completeness pressure.
  • 5Growing timeline: The timeline naturally grows longer as more events are recorded. For practitioners with many years of training, this could become quite long. A future iteration might consider collapsing older entries or showing a summary view.
  • 6Disclaimer persistence: The disclaimer text ("Belt promotions are your coach's decision") remains visible at all times, even after a promotion is recorded. This is a design principle, not just a legal precaution.
  • 7No delete action: There is intentionally no way to remove a timeline entry from this view. The permanence warning from the promotion flow is honored here. Corrections can be added as new entries.
