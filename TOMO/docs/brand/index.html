Core Philosophy

EVERY PRACTITIONER DESERVES TO SEE THEIR JOURNEY CLEARLY.

Because understanding where you've been is how you know where to go next.

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu is hard. It's supposed to be. You'll get submitted by people half your size. You'll forget techniques you drilled a hundred times. You'll plateau for months, questioning whether you're improving at all.

That difficulty is the point - it's what makes the progress meaningful.

But here's the problem: BJJ's informal promotion culture means most practitioners have no clear picture of where they stand. The path from white belt to black belt feels like walking through fog.

BJJ Progress Tracker is the flashlight.

Brand Archetype

THE DEDICATED TRAINING PARTNER

We're the purple belt who's been training for six years. Not the loudest person in the room, but the one everyone trusts to roll with on their first day and their hundredth. We remember what it felt like to be new. We've seen enough to know what matters. We're still learning too.

Personality Traits

Trait What This Means What We Avoid
Knowledgeable but humble We know the art. We respect that we're still learning. Guru energy. Talking down to users.
Warm but direct We care about your progress. We'll tell you truth. Empty positivity. Vague encouragement.
Supportive but not soft We believe in you. Growth requires discomfort. Toxic positivity. Lowering standards.
Modern but rooted Embrace technology. Respect tradition. Trendy jargon. Disrespecting old school.
Focused but flexible Have opinions. Respect your path. "One right way" thinking.

Five Pillars of Belief

1
Understanding beats memorization
Show how techniques connect - not just what, but why and when
2
The fundamentals ARE the advanced techniques
A perfect hip escape is more valuable than a flashy submission
3
Every journey is valid
Competitors, hobbyists, self-defense - all paths deserve equal respect
4
Progress is personal, not comparative
Your only opponent is yesterday's version of yourself
5
Consistency compounds
Showing up matters more than talent. Reward the work, not just wins.

Voice Attributes

Voice is consistent - it's WHO we are. These attributes define how we always communicate.

Attribute We Sound Like We Never Sound Like
Confident "Your guard retention improved 23% this month." "Maybe your guard got a little better?"
Conversational "Looks like you haven't trained in a couple weeks. Everything okay?" "ALERT: Training streak broken."
Encouraging "100 sessions logged. That's serious commitment." "OMG you're AMAZING!!!"
Clear "3 techniques left for blue belt requirements." "You're making great progress!"
Grounded "Most practitioners spend 2-3 years at blue belt." "Unlock purple belt in just 6 months!"

Tone by Context

Tone adjusts based on context - it's HOW we respond to the moment.

ACHIEVEMENT

Celebratory, proud

"50 sessions this year. You're building something real."

STRUGGLE

Empathetic, honest

"Plateaus are part of the path. Your last sessions focused on guard - that's deliberate work."

INACTIVITY

Curious, non-judgmental

"It's been a couple weeks. Life happens - ready to get back on the mat?"

ERROR/ISSUE

Calm, solution-focused

"Something went wrong saving your session. Let's try again - your notes aren't lost."

INJURY

Caring, practical

"Knee injury logged. We'll track your recovery so you know when you're ready."

COMPETITION

Focused, serious

"12 days until competition. Here's your recent training focus."

Copy Examples

Empty States

DON'T

"No training sessions yet! Get started on your BJJ journey today!"

DO

"No sessions logged yet. Log your first training after class."

Success Messages

DON'T

"Awesome job! You're crushing it!"

DO

"Session logged. 47 sessions this year."

Error Messages

DON'T

"Oops! Something went wrong!"

DO

"Couldn't save your session. Check your connection and try again."

Achievement Messages

DON'T

"Achievement unlocked: Training Warrior!"

DO

"10-week training streak. Consistency is paying off."

Terminology

WORDS WE USE

Journey - Process over destination (sparingly)
Progress - Measurable and personal
Session - Neutral term for training
Log / Logged - Professional action verb
Practitioner - Inclusive for all levels
Consistency - Core value, research-backed
Insight(s) - For analytics/patterns

WORDS WE AVOID

Level up - Gaming language
Unlock / Achievement - Gamification
Crush it / Kill it - Overhyped
Warrior / Beast - Ego-driven
Easy / Quick - False promises
Secret / Hack - No shortcuts exist
Dominate - We emphasize growth
