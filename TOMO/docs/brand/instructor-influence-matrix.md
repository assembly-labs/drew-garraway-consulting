## Pedagogy Type Classification

### Systems-Based
**Representatives:** John Danaher, Darragh O'Conaill

**Characteristics:**
- Techniques connect to larger systems
- Constraint-based learning
- Understanding WHY, not just HOW
- Positional hierarchies and decision trees

**App Application:**
- Show technique relationships in the library
- Visualize position flows and connections
- "Why it works" explanations in technique details
- System-level progress tracking (not just individual moves)

---

### Fundamentals-First
**Representatives:** Roger Gracie, Fabio Gurgel

**Characteristics:**
- Master basics before advancing
- Defense before offense
- Pressure, control, efficiency over flash
- "The basics ARE the advanced techniques"

**App Application:**
- Emphasize foundational techniques in curriculum
- Track fundamental proficiency before suggesting advanced content
- Never suggest skipping basics for shortcuts
- Celebrate mastery of simple movements

---

### High-Performance Competition
**Representatives:** Andre Galvao, Leo Vieira

**Characteristics:**
- Excellence requires measurement
- Mindset and culture matter as much as technique
- Build systems that scale success
- Data-driven training optimization

**App Application:**
- Provide meaningful metrics and analytics
- Competition preparation features
- Mindset/energy tracking
- Team and academy-level insights

---

### Technical Precision
**Representatives:** Mikey Musumeci, Mayssa Bastos

**Characteristics:**
- Details matter at every level
- Elite technique is teachable - demystify mastery
- Inclusive excellence: everyone can improve
- Micro-adjustments over macro changes

**App Application:**
- Support detailed technique notes
- Proficiency stages that capture subtle improvements
- Never assume "good enough" - always room to refine
- Make elite concepts accessible to all levels

---

### Motivational/Mindset
**Representatives:** Tom DeBlass

**Characteristics:**
- Mental resilience is trainable
- BJJ is for everyone - democratize access
- The struggle IS the path
- Community and support networks

**App Application:**
- Acknowledge difficulty without discouraging
- Celebrate consistency, not just results
- Support emotional/mental state tracking
- Inclusive messaging for all body types, ages, backgrounds

---

### Adaptive/Practical
**Representatives:** Renzo Gracie

**Characteristics:**
- Honor tradition while evolving
- Bridge old and new
- Real-world application grounds sport technique
- Continuous adaptation to changing landscape

**App Application:**
- Respect traditional terminology and lineage
- Support both sport and self-defense contexts
- Acknowledge evolution of the art
- Balance innovation with respect for fundamentals

## Voice Integration Notes

When writing copy or AI assistant responses, channel these principles:

### Cite Instructors When Relevant
- "As John Danaher often emphasizes, techniques should connect to systems..."
- "Roger Gracie's approach suggests mastering the basics first..."
- "Following the high-performance model used at Atos..."

### Respect All Pedagogies
- Don't favor one approach over another
- Acknowledge that different methods work for different practitioners
- The user's coach's philosophy takes precedence

### Match Instructor Philosophy to Feature Context
- Technique library: Systems-based thinking
- Belt progress: Fundamentals-first messaging
- Competition prep: High-performance focus
- Recovery/injury: Mindset/motivational approach
