The Next Phase: Depth Over Width

Two features that transform TOMO from a training logger into a training intelligence platform. Here's why they matter, how they work, and what they unlock.

Drew -- here's the pitch.

TOMO captures what happened in training. That's table stakes. The product audit exposed a critical gap: we don't capture why it mattered, and we don't do anything with what we already know about the user's weaknesses.

These two features close both gaps simultaneously. Reflections fills the qualitative data void -- thoughts, feelings, coaching takeaways, breakthroughs -- that makes Insights actually feel like coaching. Video Recommendations turns all that training data into action -- showing practitioners exactly what to study based on what they're struggling with.

Together, they create a flywheel: log sessions (quantitative) + reflect (qualitative) + get personalized video picks (actionable) + train better + log again. That's retention mechanics that no competitor has. That's a moat.

Feature 1 / FEAT-010
Free-Form Reflections Journal
Qualitative Data

The problem: structured extraction kills nuance.

When a user says "I finally understood why my sweeps were failing -- my coach pointed out I wasn't breaking the grip first, and once I started doing that I swept three blue belts in a row," the current system extracts: sweep (1), closed guard (1), sparring (3 rounds).

The breakthrough moment? Gone. The coach's specific feedback? Gone. The emotional context that this was a turning point in their training? Gone.

That's the data that makes coaching insights valuable. Without it, Insights can tell you what you did but never what it meant to you.

How it works

  • Voice or text, same as logging. Tap record, talk freely. No structure, no categories, no forms. Or type if you prefer. Reuses the existing voice pipeline -- zero new infrastructure.
  • Session-attached or standalone. Link a reflection to a specific session ("here's what I was thinking after Tuesday's class") or create a standalone entry ("reflecting on my training this week"). Your choice, every time.
  • AI reads between the lines. After saving, AI generates 3-5 theme tags (frustration, breakthrough, coach-feedback, technique-focus, mental-game) and a short summary. These tags are what Insights consumes -- so your weekly debrief actually knows what you've been feeling, not just what you've been drilling.
  • Privacy-first. Reflections are the most personal data in the app. Row-level security scoped to your account. No one else sees them. Ever.

User experience scenarios

Scenario 1: Post-Training Debrief

Marcus just finished an intense open mat. He sits in his car, opens TOMO, logs his session (90 seconds). Then he taps "Add Reflection" and talks for 40 seconds about how he finally hit the sweep his coach has been teaching. He mentions that the detail was breaking the grip first. He saves and drives home. On Sunday, his weekly Insight says: "You had a breakthrough with sweeps from closed guard this week -- you identified grip breaking as the missing piece. Keep testing that detail in different scenarios."

Scenario 2: Standalone Weekly Reflection

Sofia sits down on Sunday evening and opens TOMO. She's not logging a session -- she's thinking about her week. She types a few sentences: "I'm getting frustrated with my guard recovery. Every time I get passed I feel stuck. Need to ask coach about this." The following week's Insight picks up on this: "Guard recovery has been a recurring concern. You mentioned frustration with getting passed in 3 of the last 4 weeks. Consider asking your coach for specific re-guard drills from bottom side control."

Scenario 3: Coach Feedback Capture

After class, David's coach pulls him aside and gives him specific advice: "Stop reaching for underhooks from bottom half guard. Frame first, create space, then establish the underhook." David opens TOMO and voice-records the feedback immediately. Three months later, when David is working half guard again, his quarterly Insight surfaces: "Your coach emphasized framing before underhooks from half guard back in April. Are you still applying that?"

Why this matters for the business

  • Retention moat. Once a user has 20+ reflections in TOMO, they have a personal training journal they can't replicate elsewhere. That's lock-in through value, not friction.
  • Insights differentiation. Every competitor can count techniques. No one is incorporating qualitative emotional and coaching context into AI-generated debriefs. This is the feature that makes TOMO's Insights feel like they come from a coach who actually knows you.
  • Data flywheel. More qualitative data = better insights = more engagement = more data. Each reflection makes the next one more valuable because the AI has more context.
  • Low build cost, high leverage. Reuses the voice pipeline, the AI extraction infrastructure, and the existing UI patterns. The new work is a database table, one edge function, and prompt updates. Engineering effort is small relative to the value it creates.
Feature 2 / FEAT-011
AI-Powered Video Recommendations
Actionable Intelligence

The problem: practitioners don't know what to watch.

YouTube has tens of thousands of BJJ instructionals. Paid platforms add thousands more. But finding the right video for your current level, your current weaknesses, and what your gym is working on this month? That's a search problem most practitioners give up on.

They either watch whatever the algorithm suggests (rarely relevant), watch content above their level (confusing and counterproductive), or don't watch at all (missed learning opportunity).

TOMO already has the answer. We know what techniques they drilled, what submissions they got caught in, what their coach is teaching, and what belt they are. We just aren't using that data to help them yet.

How it works

  • Training data drives everything. The AI analyzes sessions from the last 2 weeks: techniques drilled, positions practiced, submissions received (weaknesses), lesson topics, belt level, training goals, and reflection themes from FEAT-010.
  • Curated library, not YouTube search. Recommendations come from a hand-vetted video library. Every video has been reviewed for quality and tagged by technique, position, belt-appropriateness, type (instructional, drill, competition breakdown), and duration. No junk.
  • Every recommendation has a "why." Not just "watch this video." Instead: "You got caught in guillotines twice this week -- this 6-minute Danaher video covers the chin tuck defense that works at every belt level." The recommendation is connected to their data.
  • Belt-filtered automatically. A white belt never sees purple belt content. The system matches content difficulty to the user's experience level without them having to filter anything.
  • Feedback loop. Thumbs up/down on each recommendation. The system learns: this user likes short drill videos, not long seminars. This user doesn't like this instructor's teaching style. Future recommendations improve.

User experience scenarios

Scenario 1: Weakness-Based Recommendation

Jake has been getting caught in triangles from closed guard three times in the last two weeks. On Sunday, his weekly Insight mentions the pattern. Below the Insight, in the "Watch This" section, the first recommendation is: "Triangle Defense Fundamentals -- 7 min. Recommended because you've been caught in triangles 3 times recently. This covers the posture and hand positioning that prevents the initial lock." Jake watches it Monday night. On Tuesday, he defends two triangle attempts.

Scenario 2: Classroom-Aligned Content

Elena's gym is doing a half guard month. Her last 4 sessions all include half guard techniques. The recommendation engine picks up on this and surfaces: "Half Guard Sweeps for Advanced Practitioners -- 12 min. Your gym is focused on half guard and you've been drilling sweeps from this position. This video adds the underhook-to-dogfight sequence you haven't tried yet."

Scenario 3: Cold Start for New Users

Ryan just signed up. He's a white belt with 2 sessions logged. Not enough data for personalized picks. Instead, he sees: "Essential White Belt Concepts" -- a curated starter pack of 5 foundational videos covering posture, hip escapes, and basic guard concepts. As he logs more sessions, the recommendations become personalized.

Why this matters for the business

  • Engagement between sessions. Right now TOMO only engages users on training days (logging) and Sundays (Insights). Video recommendations give them a reason to open the app on rest days. That's a 3-4x increase in potential touch points per week.
  • Perceived value leap. Logging and insights are useful. Personalized video coaching is valuable. This is the feature that makes a user say "I can't train without this app" instead of "this is a nice logging tool."
  • Revenue runway. Phase 3 introduces instructor partnerships and affiliate links. A BJJ Fanatics affiliate program on targeted recommendations is high-conversion, low-friction revenue. We're not there yet, but the architecture supports it.
  • Competitive moat. No BJJ app connects training data to video curation. Technique trackers don't recommend. YouTube doesn't know your belt level. This is a new category.
  • Reflections synergy. Video Recs work without Reflections, but they're dramatically better with them. A reflection that says "I'm frustrated with my guard recovery" triggers guard recovery video recommendations the next week. The two features amplify each other.
Strategic Value
The TOMO Flywheel

These features don't just add functionality. They create a self-reinforcing loop that gets more valuable with every session.

1
Train -- user goes to class, rolls, drills.
|
2
Log -- voice capture in 90 seconds. Structured data extracted (techniques, positions, rounds).
|
3
Reflect -- capture what mattered. Thoughts, feelings, coaching notes, breakthroughs. Qualitative data flows in.
|
4
Insights -- weekly debrief now has both the what and the why. Patterns surface. Coaching feels personal.
|
5
Watch -- AI picks the right videos for your weaknesses, level, and focus. You study between sessions.
|
6
Train better -- next session, you apply what you watched. Log the improvement. The cycle deepens.

The compounding effect

Every revolution of the flywheel adds data. More data = better insights = better recommendations = faster improvement = stronger habit = more data. After 3 months on this loop, a user's TOMO is so personalized to their training that switching to any other app would feel like starting over. That's organic retention, not lock-in through dark patterns.

Feature Synergy
Better Together
Intelligence Layer

Reflections make video recommendations smarter.

Without Reflections

Video engine sees: user drilled kimura 3 times, got submitted by armbar 2 times. Recommends: kimura instructional + armbar defense. Accurate but generic.

With Reflections

Video engine sees the same session data PLUS a reflection that says: "My coach told me I keep leaving my arm exposed when going for the kimura. I need to tighten my elbow before finishing." Recommends: "Common Kimura Finishing Mistakes -- 4 min. Your coach flagged arm exposure during kimura attempts. This video covers the elbow positioning detail." Specific, personal, and actionable.

This is the difference between "here's a video about that technique" and "here's a video about the exact thing your coach told you to fix." That's coaching. That's what no other app can do.

The north star metric

Users who both reflect and watch recommended videos in a given week. That's the full flywheel turning. If we get 25% of active users doing both within 60 days of launch, we've built something that's not a logger -- it's a training platform.

Scope Discipline
What we're deliberately not building
  • No social features. Reflections are private. No sharing, no coach visibility, no team feed. Privacy is the reason people will be honest in reflections.
  • No video hosting. We link and recommend. We don't re-host, download, or redistribute anyone's content. We're a recommendation layer, not a content platform.
  • No monetization yet. Affiliate links, instructor partnerships, and premium content are Phase 3. We build the value first, then the revenue model.
  • No guided prompts. Reflections are freeform on purpose. Templates and prompts kill the journal impulse. If someone wants structure, they'll create their own.
  • No algorithmic YouTube search. The video library is hand-curated. Quality over quantity. 50 vetted videos beat 5,000 random ones.
Build Sequence
Recommended execution order
Phase 1

Reflections -- Core

Database migration, voice/text input, save/edit/delete, session attachment. No AI tags yet -- just raw capture. Ship to TestFlight and validate that testers actually reflect.

Phase 2

Reflections -- AI Tags + Insights Integration

Edge function for tag generation and summarization. Update weekly/monthly insight prompts to include reflection data. This is where Insights quality takes a leap.

Phase 3

Video Library -- Curation Sprint

Manually curate 50-100 high-quality videos. Tag each with technique, position, belt range, type. Focus on the top 20 techniques from the technique tree. This is content work, not engineering.

Phase 4

Video Recommendations -- MVP

Database tables, recommendation edge function, Insights tab "Watch This" section, feedback loop. Ship to TestFlight. Measure tap-through rate and feedback quality.

Why Reflections first

Reflections make Video Recommendations better. Building Reflections first means that by the time Video Recs ship, we have qualitative data flowing into the recommendation engine from day one. Ship the data producer before the data consumer.

The bottom line

TOMO logs training. With Reflections and Video Recommendations, TOMO coaches training. That's the product we should be building. These two features are the shortest path from "useful app" to "indispensable training partner."
