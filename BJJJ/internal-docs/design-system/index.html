Post-Training UX Guidelines

User State: "Fat-Fingered & Exhausted"

Our users are logging sessions immediately after training. This means:

PHYSICAL STATE

  • Sweaty, slippery fingers
  • Hands swollen from gripping
  • Trembling from exertion
  • Reduced fine motor control
  • Often standing, one-handed

MENTAL STATE

  • Cognitive fatigue
  • Impatient (want to shower)
  • Memory fading fast
  • Low tolerance for complexity
  • Easily distracted

Tap-First Input Philosophy

Reduce typing to near-zero. Exhausted users can't type accurately. Prefer taps over keyboards:

Duration Text input "90" Preset buttons: 30 | 60 | 90 | 120
Date Date picker Today (default) | Yesterday | Pick...

Techniques Search + type Recent/Favorites first, then search

Post-Training UX Checklist

BEFORE SHIPPING ANY ENTRY FLOW:

□ Can this be completed in 3 taps or less?
□ Are all touch targets 56px+ for entry flows?
□ Is there 16px+ spacing between targets?
□ Does it work one-handed (bottom-heavy layout)?
□ Is typing reduced to optional notes only?
□ Are smart defaults pre-selected?
□ Is success feedback obvious and immediate?
□ Does it auto-save to prevent data loss?

Brand Philosophy

Core Philosophy

EVERY PRACTITIONER DESERVES TO SEE THEIR JOURNEY CLEARLY.

Because understanding where you've been is how you know where to go next.

The Archetype: The Dedicated Training Partner

We're the purple belt who's been training for six years. Not the loudest person in the room, but the one everyone trusts to roll with on their first day and their hundredth. We remember what it felt like to be new. We've seen enough to know what matters. We're still learning too.

Five Pillars

1
Understanding beats memorization
Show how techniques connect - not just what, but why and when
2
The fundamentals ARE the advanced techniques
A perfect hip escape is more valuable than a flashy submission
3
Every journey is valid
Competitors, hobbyists, self-defense - all paths deserve equal respect
4
Progress is personal, not comparative
Your only opponent is yesterday's version of yourself
5
Consistency compounds
Showing up matters more than talent. Reward the work, not just wins.

Voice Attributes

Attribute We Sound Like We Never Sound Like
Confident "Your guard retention improved 23% this month." "Maybe your guard got a little better?"
Conversational "Looks like you haven't trained in a couple weeks. Everything okay?" "ALERT: Training streak broken."
Encouraging "100 sessions logged. That's serious commitment." "OMG you're AMAZING!!!"
Clear "3 techniques left for blue belt requirements." "You're making great progress!"
Grounded "Most practitioners spend 2-3 years at blue belt." "Unlock purple belt in just 6 months!"

Terminology

WORDS WE USE

Journey - Process over destination (use sparingly)
Progress - Measurable and personal
Session - Neutral term for training
Log / Logged - Professional action verb
Practitioner - Inclusive for all levels
Consistency - Core value, research-backed
Insight(s) - For analytics/patterns

WORDS WE AVOID

Level up - Gaming language
Unlock / Achievement - Gamification
Crush it / Kill it - Overhyped
Warrior / Beast - Ego-driven
Easy / Quick - False promises
Secret / Hack - No shortcuts exist
Dominate - We emphasize growth

Non-Negotiables

BEFORE PUBLISHING ANY COPY:

X Never gamify belt progression
X Never contradict a user's coach
X Never promise faster promotions
X Never use empty hype language
X Never disrespect the tradition
? Does this respect the coach relationship? (We amplify, never replace)
? Is this specific or vague? (Numbers > adjectives)
? Would a purple belt say this? (Knowledgeable but still learning)
? Does it respect the difficulty of BJJ? (No shortcuts, no hacks)

Instructor Influence

Our content philosophy draws from BJJ's greatest instructors:

SYSTEMS-BASED

John Danaher, Darragh O'Conaill

Show technique relationships. Understanding WHY, not just HOW.

FUNDAMENTALS-FIRST

Roger Gracie, Fabio Gurgel

Master basics before advancing. The basics ARE the advanced techniques.

HIGH-PERFORMANCE

Andre Galvao, Leo Vieira

Excellence requires measurement. Mindset and culture matter.

TECHNICAL PRECISION

Mikey Musumeci, Mayssa Bastos

Details matter. Elite technique is teachable - demystify mastery.

MOTIVATIONAL

Tom DeBlass

Mental resilience is trainable. The struggle IS the path.

ADAPTIVE

Renzo Gracie

Honor tradition while evolving. Bridge old and new.
