Executive Summary

Competitive Landscape Summary

The competitive landscape reveals a fragmented market with no dominant player serving all three stakeholder groups (market managers, vendors, and customers) through an integrated platform. Most competitors serve one or two stakeholder groups, leaving significant white space for Gather's three-sided approach.

  • Local Food Marketplace (LFM) is closest competitor in farmers market online ordering — but lacks integrated market management SaaS
  • FarmDrop mirrors Gather's hub-manager model most closely — limited to 10 markets in the Northeast
  • Market Wagon has the most funding ($14.7M) — focuses exclusively on home delivery
  • Harvie shut down at end of 2024 — creates market disruption and displaced customers (opportunity!)

Our Pricing Advantage

At 2.5% transaction fee vs Market Wagon's 25% or Harvie's 10%, Gather is dramatically cheaper for vendors. The flat SaaS model aligns with farmer preference for predictable costs.

The Gap

Market management SaaS (vendor applications, stall assignments, fee collection, SNAP/EBT processing, compliance tracking, grant reporting) is completely uncontested in this competitive set.

This validates Gather's Phase 1 strategy of leading with management tools before layering the marketplace.

Fulfillment Model Comparison

Model How It Works Used By
Hub Manager Parceling Vendors deliver bulk; hub manager splits into orders FarmDrop
Vendor Pre-Pack + Hub Vendors pre-pack orders; hub aggregates for delivery Market Wagon
Vendor Pre-Pack + Pickup Vendors pre-pack orders; ops team coordinates market-day pickup Gather
Platform-Only Software connects buyers/sellers; farms handle fulfillment LFM, Local Line, WhatsGood

Gather's vendor pre-pack + market pickup model is the most operationally efficient:

  • Avoids Market Wagon's expensive last-mile delivery
  • Avoids FarmDrop's labor-intensive hub-manager parceling
  • Adds more value than LFM's platform-only approach by providing coordination layer

Recommendations & Risks

Recommended Actions

  1. Study LFM deeply — Request a demo, understand their feature depth. LFM's $59 average order and 16-32 hour weekly time savings are the benchmarks.
  2. Position against LFM's gap — Marketing: "One platform for everything" vs LFM's ordering-only approach.
  3. Recruit Harvie refugees — 150+ farms lost their platform. Reach out through agricultural extension networks.
  4. Build market-manager case studies fast — Carlo's Berwyn experience = anchor case study no competitor can replicate.
  5. Maintain pricing advantage — 2.5% vs 25% is major. Resist commission increases; SaaS subscription is sustainable revenue.

Risks to Monitor

Watch These Risks

  • LFM expanding into management — If they add vendor applications, compliance tracking, grant reporting, they close our primary differentiation gap.
  • WhatsGood's white-label scale — Aggressive expansion could lock in market relationships before we launch.
  • Market Wagon's funding — $14.7M could fund pivot to pickup options or market management features.
