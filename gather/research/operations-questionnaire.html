SNAP/EBT Operations

21If your market accepts SNAP/EBT, describe your complete process.

How do you process transactions? Distribute tokens? Reconcile at end of day? What's most painful? How much time does it take weekly?

Example Answer
Process: 1. Customer comes to info booth with EBT card 2. We swipe card on our terminal, enter amount 3. Give customer wooden tokens ($1, $5 denominations) 4. Customer shops with tokens 5. Vendors accept tokens like cash 6. End of day: vendors return tokens to us 7. We count tokens per vendor, record amounts 8. We reimburse vendors (currently via check, once per month) Pain points: • Token counting takes 45-60 minutes after every market • Tokens get lost, damaged, miscounted • Vendors sometimes forget to return tokens • Monthly reimbursement is slow — vendors want faster payment • Tracking Market Match (bonus dollars) adds complexity • USDA reporting requirements are confusing Time: 5-6 hours per week total (market day processing + end-of-month reconciliation + reporting)
Your Answer

22Do you participate in matching programs (Market Match, Double Up Food Bucks)?

How do you track and report on these programs?

Example Answer
Yes, we participate in PA's Fresh Food Financing Initiative matching program. SNAP customers get $2 match for every $5 spent (up to $20 match per visit). Tracking: Separate colored tokens for match dollars. More token counting. Reporting: Monthly report to the funding organization showing # of SNAP customers served, total SNAP sales, total match distributed, by date. I compile this from handwritten logs. Takes about 3 hours per month, and I'm never confident the numbers are 100% accurate.

Customer Pickup

26How do customers currently pick up their pre-orders?

Example Answer
Friday curbside: Central pickup table. Customer pulls up, tells us their name, we hand them their bags. Simple but I have to be there for the full 2-hour window. Your Answer

27What happens when things go wrong at pickup?

Order isn't ready? Customer no-show? Customer wants to add items? Item needs substitution?

Example Answer
Order not ready: Customer waits (unhappy). I scramble to find the vendor or figure out what happened. Usually resolved in 10-15 minutes but damages customer trust. No-show: We hold the order until market closes. Then the vendor takes it back (usually can sell items, but not always). We've had ~5% no-show rate. For perishables, this means food waste. Want to add items: Customer just shops at market and pays separately. Pre-order and walk-up aren't integrated. Substitution needed: I try to contact customer before pickup. If I can't reach them, vendor decides (similar item, similar price). Customer can refuse substitution at pickup and get refund, but this is rare.

Wish I had: Some kind of scan or confirmation system so we know the order is complete before they leave.
