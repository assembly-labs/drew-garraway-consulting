Bottom Line

They're not our competitor. Different model, different customer. But they validate demand — and they're eyeing our territory.

Dylan Baird started this in 2014. He was an urban farmer in West Philly who couldn't grow enough. Connected with Amish farmers in Chester County who had too much. Built an aggregation + delivery business.

They buy from farmers, pack it in a warehouse in North Philly, and deliver to homes or community pickup locations. $8 delivery fee, $28 minimum.

*Revenue is an estimate from business databases (ZoomInfo, CIENCE). They're private and don't disclose.

The Key Difference

Them
Buy inventory, pack boxes, deliver
Gather
Facilitate marketplace, zero inventory

They take inventory risk. They commit to buying from farmers at season start — even if they can't sell it. That's the same model that killed Good Eggs ($200M burned) and Farmigo ($26M).

We never touch product. Vendors sell direct. We just make the transaction easier.

What They Do Well

  • Deep farmer relationships — annual crop planning, price guarantees, financed 3 greenhouses for partners
  • Flexible model — not a forced CSA box, full customization, skip anytime
  • Community programs — 8,000+ discounted boxes to underserved neighborhoods
  • Sustainability cred — returnable packaging, <1% food waste
  • 10 years of trust — that matters in local food

Where They're Weak

  • Inventory risk — one bad season could hurt them badly
  • Premium pricing — limits their addressable market
  • Delivery economics — $8 fee doesn't cover true cost
  • No market management tools — they don't serve managers at all
  • Philly-only — can't scale without warehouses everywhere
  • Small team — 10-14 people, limited bandwidth

What Their CEO Said

"Farmers' markets were not the most efficient or effective method of fresh food distribution. Oftentimes, farmers' markets make it difficult for local farmers to pursue profitability."

— Dylan Baird, CEO

He's not wrong about the problem. But his solution was to abandon markets. Ours is to fix them with technology.

Same diagnosis. Different prescription.

Their Tech

They run on Navego — a food hub platform out of Colorado. Costs them 1% of revenue.

Navego does subscriptions, inventory, delivery routing. But here's what it doesn't do:

  • No vendor applications or onboarding
  • No stall assignments or market layouts
  • No fee collection or compliance tracking
  • No grant reporting
  • No multi-vendor marketplace for physical pickup

That's the gap we fill. Nobody else is building for market managers.

Main Line Threat

They're already in Delaware County

Adjacent to the Main Line. Their expansion policy: "5+ people request it, we add a route." They have 60+ pickup locations and room to grow.

No public announcements about Berwyn or Wayne. But the infrastructure is there. If they add pickup spots at community centers or coffee shops in our territory, they get a foothold.

We need to lock in market managers first.

Different Customers

Their customer says:

"Deliver local food to my door. I don't want to go anywhere. Curate it for me."

Our customer says:

"I love the market experience. I just wish I could pre-order and see what's available before I go."

Minimal overlap. They serve convenience-first subscribers. We serve experience-first shoppers.

What To Do About It

1
Lock In Market Managers

Hit the 12+ Main Line markets before Philly Foodworks plants pickup locations. First-mover wins.

2
Document Your Playbook

Write down everything you do at Berwyn. Vendor selection, rotation, fee collection. This becomes the franchise training.

3
Build for White-Label

Platform should support multiple markets from day one. Each market = separate tenant, separate branding.

TL;DR

Philly Foodworks delivers curated boxes. We bring the farmers market experience online.

Different customers. Different economics. They take inventory risk; we don't. They need warehouses to scale; we don't.

But they validate that people want local food tech. And they're growing toward our territory.

Move fast. Sign markets. Build the platform.
