## Executive Summary

The competitive landscape reveals a **fragmented market with no dominant player** serving all three stakeholder groups (market managers, vendors, and customers) through an integrated platform. Most competitors serve one or two stakeholder groups, leaving significant white space for Gather's three-sided approach.

## Detailed Competitor Profiles

### FarmDrop

**farmdrop.us** | Maine-based | Founded 2011, expanded 2016+

#### Overview

FarmDrop is the closest operational model to Gather. It operates a multi-vendor online marketplace where customers order from multiple local farms/vendors and pick up at a central hub. A Market Hub Manager (analogous to Gather's ops team) receives bulk deliveries from vendors, parcels out individual customer orders, and coordinates weekly pickup.

#### Operational Model

- **Order window:** Weekly cycle, typically Friday-Monday ordering, Wednesday pickup
- **Hub Manager role:** Receives vendor deliveries in bulk, splits into customer orders, manages pickup logistics
- **Vendor model:** Harvest-to-order reduces waste; vendors only produce what's been pre-ordered
- **Markets:** 8 in Maine, 2 in New York; run primarily through nonprofit partners (e.g., Healthy Acadia)
- **Training:** Provides training portal for hub managers; nonprofit-focused food security mission

#### Pricing

- Vendors keep 95% of sales; 5% goes to FarmDrop + 2.9% + $0.30 Stripe processing
- Customers pay a small fulfillment fee per order
- Adding EBT payment option (in progress)

#### Implications for Gather

**Threat level: Low-Medium.** FarmDrop validates Gather's hub-manager fulfillment model but is limited geographically (Maine/NY), nonprofit-dependent, and lacks market management SaaS. Their 95/5 vendor split is more generous than industry standard but may limit platform sustainability. Gather's professional management approach and three-sided platform offer clear differentiation.

### Market Wagon

**marketwagon.com** | Indianapolis | Founded 2017 | **$14.7M raised**

#### Overview

Market Wagon is the most well-funded direct competitor, operating a hub-and-spoke delivery model. Vendors pre-pack customer orders, bring them to a central hub on delivery day, and gig drivers deliver to customers' homes. The platform aggregates 2,500+ producers across 12+ hubs in 10 states.

#### Operational Model

- **Fulfillment:** Vendors pre-pack their own orders, deliver to hub; gig drivers handle last-mile home delivery
- **No warehousing:** Only purchased items brought to hub on delivery day, eliminating inventory risk
- **Scale:** 12+ hubs across Indiana, Ohio, Kentucky, Tennessee, Michigan, and other Midwest/East states
- **Founders:** Nick Carter (farmer) + Dan Brunner (logistics engineer)
- **Acquisition:** Acquired Find Your Farmer (St. Louis) in January 2024, expanding footprint

#### Pricing & Business Model

- **Commission:** ~25% platform fee (vendors keep ~75%), compared to typical 50/50 grocery distributor split
- **Investors:** Hyde Park Venture Partners, Impact Engine, Tapas Capital
- **Revenue model:** Pure marketplace commission; no SaaS subscriptions

#### Implications for Gather

**Threat level: Medium.** Market Wagon has significant funding and scale, but their delivery-focused model fundamentally differs from Gather's market-pickup approach. Their 25% commission is steep for vendors compared to Gather's 2.5% transaction fee. Market Wagon serves convenience-driven consumers willing to pay for delivery; Gather serves community-driven shoppers who want the market experience with pre-ordering convenience. The two platforms could coexist serving different customer segments.

**Key vulnerability:** Market Wagon has no market management tools and no relationship with market managers.

### WhatsGood

**whatsgood.com** | Rhode Island | Founded 2015

#### Overview

WhatsGood operates an Instacart-like farm-to-table service with both delivery and pickup options. Founded by Matt and Erin Tortora with William Araujo, it initially served B2B (restaurants) before pivoting to consumer. The platform white-labels for farmers markets, providing a branded shopping experience.

#### Operational Model

- **Multi-channel:** Serves DC, Boston, Rhode Island, and Chicago markets with both delivery and pickup
- **White-label:** Used by 30+ Oregon farmers markets, Urban Village FM Oakland, and others as their online ordering platform
- **Pickup partners:** Uses breweries, libraries, and workplaces as pickup locations beyond market sites
- **Logistics:** Milk-run delivery model with aggregation hub
- **Scale:** 20K+ users across four metro regions

#### Implications for Gather

**Threat level: Medium.** WhatsGood's white-label approach for farmers markets is strategically similar to Gather's marketplace, and their multi-region presence shows the model works. However, they lack integrated market management tools and focus primarily on the shopping/delivery experience. Their early revenue (~$1M in first 2 years) suggests slow growth. Gather's integration of management SaaS with marketplace creates a stickier platform.

**Worth watching:** Their white-label farmers market partnerships closely.

### Local Food Marketplace (LFM)

**localfoodmarketplace.com** | Established 2009 | Bootstrapped

#### Overview

LFM is Gather's most direct competitor for farmers market online ordering. Established since 2009, it serves farms, food hubs, farmers markets, and CSAs with a comprehensive multi-vendor marketplace platform. Dozens of farmers markets use LFM for online pre-ordering with curbside pickup.

#### Key Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average order (pickup) | **$59** |
| Average order (delivery) | $161 |
| Admin time savings | 16-32 hours/week |
| Notable clients | Boise FM, Bloomington Community FM, Boulder County FM, Red Hills Small Farm Alliance |

*Note: The $59 pickup basket validates Gather's target assumption.*

#### Features

- Unlimited price lists
- Harvest/pack lists
- Route management
- QuickBooks integration
- SNAP/EBT support
- Vendor payouts

#### Pricing

- **$149/month** base (one network), prepaid annually
- **No transaction fees**
- Additional networks at custom pricing
- Success Package required for onboarding
- SMS messaging at $49/month for up to 2,000 messages

#### Strengths

LFM has the deepest feature set for multi-vendor farmers market ordering. Their no-transaction-fee model appeals to price-sensitive vendors. Their Local Food Connect product manages multi-store inventory for farms selling across multiple online stores and in-person outlets. Market managers praise the automated pick lists, producer payouts, and reporting. The team has deep domain expertise from running farms and food hubs themselves.

#### Weaknesses & Gather's Opportunity

LFM lacks integrated market management SaaS. They handle online ordering and fulfillment coordination beautifully, but don't offer:

- Vendor application management
- Stall assignments
- Compliance tracking
- Grant reporting
- Fee collection
- Communication hubs

**This is the entire Phase 1 of Gather's product.** A market using LFM still needs spreadsheets for everything else.

Additionally, LFM's interface feels dated compared to modern consumer expectations (Instacart-level UX). Gather's opportunity is to match LFM's ordering functionality while wrapping it in superior management tools and a modern consumer shopping experience.

#### Implications for Gather

**Threat level: High.** LFM is the incumbent in farmers market online ordering with proven product-market fit, strong testimonials, and established relationships. However, their narrow focus on ordering/fulfillment (not market management), dated UX, and lack of Instacart-style consumer experience leave clear differentiation opportunities.

Gather should study LFM's feature set closely as the baseline expectation from markets that have used online ordering.

**Biggest risk:** LFM expanding into market management before Gather launches.

### Harvie (SHUT DOWN End of 2024)

**harvie.farm** | Pittsburgh | Founded by Simon Huntley

#### Overview

Harvie was a CSA platform that offered customizable farm share boxes using an algorithm matching crops to member preferences. At its peak, Harvie served 150+ farms across the US and Canada, delivering 500,000+ farm boxes to 50,000+ consumers. **The platform shut down at the end of 2024, creating significant market disruption.**

#### What Made Harvie Notable

- **Personalization at scale:** Members rated crops 1-5; algorithm optimized weekly boxes to preferences
- **Retention impact:** 15-20% higher retention rates for member farms versus standard CSA
- **COVID growth:** Farmer sales up 200% during pandemic
- **Pricing:** $500 setup fee + 7% transaction fee + 3% credit card processing

#### Implications for Gather

**Opportunity:** Harvie's shutdown creates displaced farms looking for new platforms. Local Line is actively marketing as the Harvie alternative, but Gather could capture farms seeking a more integrated marketplace approach.

Harvie's failure also serves as a cautionary tale about relying solely on CSA/subscription models without broader marketplace functionality.

**The farms migrating away from Harvie represent a warm lead pool for Gather's vendor recruitment.**

### Local Line

**localline.ca** | Canadian | Chipotle-backed

#### Overview

Local Line is a farm-to-fork commerce platform serving 13,000+ farms with retail, wholesale, restaurant, grocer, and CSA sales in one platform. Backed strategically by Chipotle Mexican Grill (both customer and investor), Local Line is positioning as the comprehensive farm e-commerce solution and actively recruiting Harvie refugees.

#### Key Features

- Customizable subscriptions
- Box builder
- Weight-based selling
- Multiple price lists
- SNAP/EBT support
- Wholesale pricing
- Food hub management
- Pricing starts at $29/month

#### Implications for Gather

**Threat level: Medium-Low for marketplace, High for vendor tools.** Local Line excels at individual farm e-commerce and wholesale but doesn't operate multi-vendor marketplaces at the market level. Their food hub features compete with Gather's longer-term roadmap but not the core market-manager + consumer-marketplace offering.

The Chipotle relationship gives institutional credibility.

**Gather's differentiation:** Local Line serves farms, Gather serves markets.

## Strategic Analysis

### Fulfillment Model Comparison

The competitive landscape reveals four distinct fulfillment approaches, each with tradeoffs:

| Model | How It Works | Used By |
|-------|--------------|---------|
| **Hub Manager Parceling** | Vendors deliver in bulk; hub manager splits into customer orders | FarmDrop |
| **Vendor Pre-Pack + Hub** | Vendors pre-pack individual orders; hub aggregates for delivery | Market Wagon |
| **Vendor Pre-Pack + Pickup** | Vendors pre-pack orders; ops team coordinates market-day pickup | **Gather** |
| **Platform-Only** | Software connects buyers/sellers; farms handle all fulfillment | LFM, Local Line, WhatsGood |

Gather's vendor pre-pack + market pickup model is the most operationally efficient approach:
- Avoids Market Wagon's expensive last-mile delivery
- Avoids FarmDrop's labor-intensive hub-manager parceling
- Adds more value than LFM's platform-only approach by providing the coordination layer that makes multi-vendor pickup seamless for customers

### The Uncontested Territory

**No competitor serves all three stakeholder groups in one integrated platform.**

| Platform | Market Managers | Vendors | Consumers |
|----------|-----------------|---------|-----------|
| FarmDrop | Minimal | Yes (ordering) | Yes (shopping) |
| Market Wagon | No | Yes (delivery) | Yes (delivery) |
| WhatsGood | Minimal | Yes (ordering) | Yes (shopping) |
| LFM | Partial (ordering only) | Yes (ordering) | Yes (shopping) |
| Local Line | No | Yes (e-commerce) | Limited |
| **Gather** | **Full SaaS suite** | **Ordering + mgmt** | **Instacart-style UX** |

**Market management SaaS** (vendor applications, stall assignments, fee collection, SNAP/EBT processing, compliance tracking, grant reporting) **is completely uncontested** in this competitive set.

This validates Gather's Phase 1 strategy of leading with management tools before layering the marketplace.

## Key Takeaways & Recommendations

### Competitive Advantages to Emphasize

#### 1. Three-sided platform
No competitor integrates market management, vendor tools, and consumer marketplace. This creates compound value and switching costs that pure ordering platforms can't match.

#### 2. Market-manager relationship
Gather enters through the manager (Carlo's network), who then brings vendors and customers. Competitors enter through vendors or consumers, never managers. **This is a distribution advantage.**

#### 3. Modern consumer UX
LFM's ordering interface is functional but dated. Gather's Instacart-style shopping experience (per the design system) represents a generational leap in consumer experience for farmers market shopping.

#### 4. Pricing structure
At $49-299/month SaaS + 2.5% transaction fee, Gather is dramatically cheaper for vendors than Market Wagon's 25% or Harvie's 10% combined fees. The flat SaaS model aligns with farmer preference for predictable costs over percentage-based models.

#### 5. Vendor pre-pack model
Avoids the operational complexity and cost of hub-manager parceling (FarmDrop) or last-mile delivery (Market Wagon) while still providing seamless multi-vendor pickup for consumers.

### Risks to Monitor

| Risk | Why It Matters |
|------|----------------|
| **LFM expanding into management** | If LFM adds vendor application management, compliance tracking, and grant reporting, they could close Gather's primary differentiation gap. Their Local Food Connect product shows they're building multi-store infrastructure. |
| **WhatsGood's white-label scale** | If WhatsGood aggressively expands their white-label program to more farmers markets, they could lock in market relationships before Gather launches. |
| **Market Wagon's funding** | With $14.7M raised and acquisition activity, Market Wagon could pivot to add pickup options or market management features. Their engineering resources dwarf what Gather can deploy initially. |

---

### Recommended Actions

#### 1. Study LFM deeply
Request a demo, understand their feature depth, and ensure Gather's Phase 2 marketplace matches or exceeds their ordering capabilities. LFM's $59 average order and 16-32 hour weekly time savings are the benchmarks to beat.

#### 2. Position against LFM's gap
Marketing should emphasize that Gather replaces both their spreadsheets AND their ordering platform in one tool. **"One platform for everything"** versus LFM's ordering-only approach.

#### 3. Recruit Harvie refugees
150+ farms lost their platform. These are warm leads who already understand online selling and need a new home. Reach out through agricultural extension networks.

#### 4. Build market-manager case studies fast
No competitor has market-manager testimonials because no competitor serves them. Carlo's Berwyn experience should be the anchor case study that no competitor can replicate.

#### 5. Maintain pricing advantage
The 2.5% transaction fee versus Market Wagon's 25% or Harvie's 10% is a major selling point. Resist pressure to increase commissions; the SaaS subscription is the sustainable revenue base.
