Questions We're Hearing

Is this replacing librarians?

Absolutely not. This tool handles routine discovery tasks (like explaining Boolean operators or searching multiple systems) so you can spend time on complex research questions, readers' advisory, community programming, and the human connection that defines librarianship. Think of it as taking mundane work off your plate, not taking your job. Librarians remain essential for nuance, judgment, and the kind of personalized help that technology can't replicate.

What about patron privacy?

Privacy is foundational, not an afterthought. We're building a zero-logging architecture where patron searches are never stored in any database, never associated with accounts, never tracked across sessions. We meet ALA Library Privacy Guidelines and will undergo third-party privacy audits to prove it. Your IT department can review our architecture. If we can't meet your privacy standards, we don't deserve to serve libraries.

How does this address concerns about AI?

We share your skepticism of overhyped technology. That's why we're building with librarians to ensure this tool: (1) never invents information (recommendations come from your actual catalog); (2) transparently hands off complex questions to reference librarians; (3) respects patron privacy with zero data retention; and (4) augments librarian expertise rather than claiming to replace it. You validate every claim we make about functionality and values alignment. If it doesn't meet library standards, we fix it or we don't ship it.

What's the time commitment?

Approximately 4-6 hours per month: one 60-90 minute virtual meeting, plus occasional testing with your patrons (30-60 minutes) and brief feedback surveys (10-15 minutes). We know you're already stretched thin, so we structure participation to respect your time. Meetings are recorded if you can't attend live. You can adjust involvement based on your capacity in any given month.

Will libraries have to pay for this?

Cohort members receive free access during development (12-16 weeks) and significant founder's pricing if you choose to continue (20-30% discount). We're building a sustainable SaaS model with tiered pricing based on library size: $5K-$15K annually for most public libraries, with LSTA grant support strategies. Cohort participation is completely free with no obligation. If the tool doesn't prove its value during the pilot, you walk away having helped shape something for the broader library community.

Can I drop out if it's not working?

Yes, absolutely. This is a voluntary partnership. If your schedule changes, if the tool doesn't meet expectations, or if participation isn't valuable to you, simply let us know. No hard feelings, no strings attached. We want engaged partners who see the potential, not obligated participants. Your feedback about why it's not working would be helpful, but there's zero pressure to stay if it's not the right fit.
